What is Tonsillitis?

Your tonsils are the two small, oval pads of tissue at the back of your throat. They act as "guards" for your immune system, trapping germs that enter through your nose and mouth.

Sometimes, these guards get overwhelmed by bacteria or viruses. When this happens, they become swollen, red, and painful. This infection is called tonsillitis. While it is very common in children, adults in Nepal suffer from it too.

Common Symptoms: How to Tell if You Have It

Symptoms often appear suddenly. You might need a health checkup if you notice:

  • Severe throat pain: It hurts to swallow water or food.
  • Swollen tonsils: They look red or have white/yellow spots.
  • Fever: A temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher.
  • Swollen glands: The sides of your neck feel tender.
  • Bad breath: Caused by the infection hiding in the tonsils.
  • Voice changes: Your voice sounds scratchy or muffled.

Note for Parents: If your young child is refusing to eat, drooling excessively, or acting unusually fussy, they may have a sore throat they cannot tell you about.

Why Does My Sore Throat Keep Coming Back?

Many patients visit Maria Hospital asking why their throat pain never seems to go away completely. This is often due to Recurrent Tonsillitis.

Bacteria can sometimes create a hiding spot called a "biofilm" on your tonsils. This film protects the germs from antibiotics and your immune system. When you get tired, stressed, or catch a small cold, these hidden bacteria "wake up" and cause a new infection.

Other triggers can include:

  • Seasonal allergies
  • Pollution or smoking
  • Acid reflux (Gastric issues)

Diagnosis and Treatment at Maria Hospital

To treat you properly, our doctors first need to know if your infection is caused by a virus or bacteria.

1. Accurate Diagnosis

When you come for a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, we may perform:

  • Physical Exam: Checking for swelling and redness.
  • Throat Swab: A quick test to check for "Strep throat" bacteria.

2. Effective Treatment Options

Once we know the cause, we create a personalized plan for you:

  • Home Care (For Viral Infections): If it is a virus, antibiotics won't help. We recommend rest, hydration (plenty of warm water or soup), and salt-water gargles.
  • Antibiotics (For Bacterial Infections): If you have a bacterial infection, we will prescribe antibiotics. Important: You must finish the full course of medicine to prevent the infection from returning.
  • Surgery (Tonsillectomy): If you suffer from severe, frequent infections or breathing issues (snoring/sleep apnea), we may recommend removing the tonsils. Modern surgery at Maria Hospital is safe, quick, and usually allows you to go home the same day.

When Should You See a Doctor?

Don't ignore persistent pain. Book a doctor consultation if:

  • Your sore throat lasts more than 2 days.
  • You have a high fever.
  • The problem keeps coming back.

Seek emergency help immediately if you have difficulty breathing, cannot swallow saliva (drooling), or have a stiff neck.

Protect Yourself and Your Family

Tonsillitis is contagious. Keep your family in Nepal safe by:

  • Washing hands often, especially before eating.
  • Not sharing water bottles, spoons, or toothbrushes.
  • Changing your toothbrush after you recover from a sore throat.
