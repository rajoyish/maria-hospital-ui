Vertigo, Dizziness and Balance Disorders Treatment

A balance disorder is a condition that makes you feel unsteady or dizzy. This can happen whether you are lying in bed, sitting in a chair, or walking down the street. Doctors usually group these feelings into three main types:

  • Vertigo: A specific feeling that you—or the room around you—is spinning, even when you are standing still.
  • Dizziness: A general feeling of being lightheaded, woozy, or faint.
  • Imbalance: A feeling of unsteadiness, like you might fall over while walking.

How Your Body Keeps Balance

Your brain acts like a control center. To keep you steady, it collects information from three main areas:

  • Your Eyes: They tell you where you are.
  • Your Muscles & Joints: They tell your brain if you are moving or standing.
  • Your Inner Ear: This is the most important part. It detects gravity and head movements.

When these signals don't match (for example, your inner ear thinks you are spinning, but your eyes say you are sitting still), you experience vertigo.

Common Symptoms

Aside from the "spinning" sensation, you might also experience:

  • Nausea or vomiting.
  • Blurred vision.
  • Stumbling or trouble walking straight.
  • Confusion or anxiety.

Top Causes of Balance Problems

Most balance issues start in the inner ear. Here are the most common causes our doctors see:

1. BPPV (The "Ear Crystal" Problem)

This is the most common cause of vertigo in adults. It happens when tiny calcium crystals inside your ear break loose and move to the wrong place.

What it feels like: Short, intense spinning when you move your head (like rolling over in bed or looking up).

2. Viral Infections (Labyrinthitis)

Sometimes, a simple virus (like the flu) can cause inflammation in your inner ear.

What it feels like: Sudden, severe dizziness that can last for days, often with nausea.

3. Meniere’s Disease

A chronic condition caused by fluid buildup in the inner ear.

What it feels like: Long episodes of vertigo (20 minutes or more), ringing in the ears (tinnitus), and hearing changes.

4. Migraines

Migraines are not just headaches; they can cause dizziness too.

What it feels like: Dizziness triggered by light, sound, or motion, sometimes without any head pain.

Diagnosis at Maria Hospital

Finding the root cause of your dizziness is the first step toward relief. When you visit Maria Hospital for a doctor consultation, our specialists (ENTs or audiologists) may use:

  • Hearing Tests: To check the connection between your hearing and balance.
  • Balance Tests: Using special goggles or platforms to see how your inner ear reacts to movement.
  • Position Tests: A doctor may carefully move your head to see if it triggers vertigo (confirming BPPV).

Treatment Options in Nepal

The good news is that most balance disorders are highly treatable. Depending on your diagnosis, your treatment plan may include:

  • Repositioning Maneuvers: A doctor guides your head through specific movements to move ear crystals back to the right place.
  • Vestibular Rehabilitation: Specialized exercises to retrain your brain and improve stability.
  • Medication: To control nausea or manage conditions like migraines.
  • Lifestyle Changes: Simple adjustments like staying hydrated or reducing salt.

When to Seek Emergency Care

While most vertigo is not dangerous, it can sometimes signal a serious issue like a stroke. Please visit the hospital immediately if your dizziness comes with:

  • A sudden, severe headache.
  • Chest pain.
  • Double vision.
  • Slurred speech or weakness in the arms/legs.
  • High fever.
