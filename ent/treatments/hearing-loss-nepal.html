Hearing Loss and Age-Related Hearing Problems Treatment

Hearing loss is a very common condition. While it can affect anyone, it is most often linked to getting older. If you or a family member are finding it harder to hear, you are not alone.

At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, we believe that understanding why your hearing is changing is the first step to feeling better. With the right diagnosis and care, you can improve your communication and enjoy a better quality of life.

What Is Age-Related Hearing Loss?

As we age, our bodies change, and that includes our ears. Medically, this is called presbycusis. It is a gradual loss of hearing that happens slowly over time.

  • It is common: About one in three adults over the age of 65 has some hearing loss.
  • It is slow: Because it happens gradually, you might not notice it immediately.
  • It affects both ears: Usually, hearing fades in both ears at the same time.

Common Symptoms: Is This Happening to You?

Often, family members notice the problem before the patient does. You might need a health checkup if you experience these signs:

  • Conversation struggles: It is hard to understand people, especially in noisy places like restaurants or family gatherings.
  • Volume issues: You turn the TV, radio, or mobile phone volume much higher than others do.
  • Mumbling voices: It feels like people are mumbling or not speaking clearly.
  • Missing sounds: You cannot hear high-pitched sounds, like birds chirping or the voices of women and children.
  • Ringing ears: You hear a ringing or buzzing sound (tinnitus) in your ears.
  • Repeating words: You frequently have to ask people to repeat words.

Why Does Hearing Loss Happen?

Hearing loss occurs when parts of the ear are damaged or blocked. The two main types are:

  1. Permanent Hearing Loss (Sensorineural): This is usually caused by aging or damage to the inner ear nerves.
  2. Blockage (Conductive): This happens when sound cannot get through due to earwax, fluid, or infection. This can often be fixed with simple treatment.

Key Risk Factors

Aside from aging, other things can affect your hearing:

  • Loud Noise: Long-term exposure to Kathmandu traffic, construction noise, or loud music.
  • Health Conditions: Diabetes and high blood pressure are linked to hearing issues.
  • Genetics: Sometimes, hearing loss runs in the family.

How We Diagnose Hearing Loss at Maria Hospital

If you think you have hearing loss, do not wait. A proper doctor consultation is essential. At Maria Hospital, our specialists (audiologists) use safe and simple methods to check your ears:

  • Physical Exam: We look inside your ear to check for earwax or infection.
  • Hearing Tests: You wear headphones and listen for sounds at different pitches to measure your hearing ability.

Treatment and Management

While age-related hearing loss cannot always be cured, it can be managed very well.

  • Hearing Aids: Small electronic devices that make sounds louder and clearer.
  • Earwax Removal: If a blockage is the problem, our doctors can safely remove the wax to restore your hearing immediately.
  • Assistive Devices: Special tools for phones and TVs to help you hear better.

Tips to Protect Your Ears

You can take steps today to prevent further damage:

  • Avoid very loud noises when possible.
  • Wear earplugs if you work in noisy construction or industrial areas in Nepal.
  • Keep the volume down on headphones.
  • Manage your blood pressure and blood sugar levels.

When to Contact a Doctor

Hearing loss can make you feel lonely or isolated from your family. If hearing problems are affecting your daily life, or if you have sudden hearing loss, seek medical attention immediately.
