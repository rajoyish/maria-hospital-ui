What Is Happening to Your Nose?

Sneezing, a runny nose, and congestion often happen at the same time, but they are actually different ways your body protects itself:

  • Sneezing: This is your body’s reflex to kick out "intruders" like dust, pollen, or viruses from your nose.
  • Runny Nose: Your body produces extra mucus (liquid) to wash away germs. It’s a sign your immune system is working hard.
  • Nasal Congestion (Stuffy Nose): Many people think this is just a blockage of mucus. However, it is actually caused by the swelling of tissues inside your nose. This narrows your airways, making it hard to breathe.

Common Causes of Nasal Symptoms

Your nose acts as a filter for the air you breathe. Because of this, many things can trigger symptoms. Here are the most common causes we see during a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital:

1. Viral Infections

The most common causes are the common cold, the flu, or COVID-19. Your body creates inflammation and mucus to trap the virus.

Symptoms:

  • Sneezing
  • Thick yellow or green mucus
  • Sore throat
  • Tiredness

2. Allergies (Allergic Rhinitis)

In Nepal, dust and pollen are major triggers. If your immune system reacts too strongly to these harmless things, you have allergies.

Symptoms:

  • Watery runny nose
  • Itchy eyes
  • Frequent sneezing

3. Environmental Irritants

Sometimes, your nose is simply irritated by your surroundings.

  • Cold Air: Dry, cold air can dry out your nose, causing it to run.
  • Pollution and Smoke: The dust in Kathmandu, vehicle smoke, or strong perfumes can trigger sneezing.
  • Spicy Food: Eating hot or spicy Nepali food can sometimes make your nose run immediately.

4. Other Health Factors

  • Nasal Polyps: Small, painless growths inside the nose that block air.
  • Medications: Some blood pressure medicines or using nasal sprays for too long can cause congestion.

Simple Home Treatments

In many cases, these symptoms will go away on their own. You can try these simple home remedies to feel better:

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to keep mucus thin and easy to drain.
  • Use Saline Rinses: Saltwater sprays are safe and effective for washing out dust and allergens.
  • Humidifiers: Using a humidifier adds moisture to the air, which helps soothe dry nasal passages.
  • Avoid Triggers: If you have allergies, try to wear a mask in dusty areas and keep windows closed during high-pollen seasons.

When Should You Visit Maria Hospital?

While a stuffy nose is usually not dangerous, sometimes you need professional medical care. You should book a health checkup or doctor consultation at Maria Hospital if:

  • Symptoms last too long: Your congestion or runny nose lasts more than 10 days.
  • High Fever: You have a high fever along with nasal discharge.
  • Pain and Colored Mucus: You have green or yellow mucus with pain in your face/sinuses (this could be a bacterial infection requiring diagnosis).
  • Breathing Trouble: You are wheezing or struggling to breathe.
  • One-sided Symptoms: Discharge comes from only one side of the nose, or smells bad.
