Nasal Polyps Treatment

Do you feel like you have a cold that just won’t go away? Are you constantly struggling with a blocked or stuffy nose? You might be experiencing nasal polyps.

While the name might sound scary, there is no need to panic. These are common, non-cancerous growths. At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, we are here to help you understand this condition and provide the right treatment so you can breathe freely again.

What Are Nasal Polyps?

Nasal polyps are soft, painless growths inside your nose or sinuses. They often look like small teardrops or grapes. They are benign (not cancer) and do not increase your risk of cancer.

Small polyps might not bother you at all. However, if they grow larger or form in groups, they can block your nasal passages. This makes it hard to breathe, smell, or sleep comfortably.

Common Signs and Symptoms

Since polyps grow slowly, many people in Nepal mistake them for a long-lasting cold or seasonal allergies. You should consider a doctor consultation if you have:

  • Blocked or stuffy nose: The most annoying symptom that doesn't get better.
  • Runny nose: Constant mucus dripping down the back of your throat (postnasal drip).
  • Loss of smell or taste: Food might taste bland, or you can’t smell properly.
  • Facial pressure: Feeling fullness or mild pain in your forehead and face.
  • Snoring: Trouble breathing at night can lead to poor sleep.

Note: If these symptoms last for more than 12 weeks, it is likely chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps.

Why Do They Happen?

Doctors aren't 100% sure why some people get them, but they are caused by long-term (chronic) inflammation in the nose. They are most common in adults aged 30 to 40.

You might be at higher risk if you have:

  • Asthma
  • Frequent sinus infections
  • Allergies (Hay Fever)
  • Sensitivity to Aspirin
  • Cystic Fibrosis

Diagnosis at Maria Hospital

If you are worried about your symptoms, a quick health checkup at Maria Hospital can give you answers. Our specialists in Kathmandu use simple methods to provide an accurate diagnosis:

  • Physical Exam: We look inside your nose with a simple light.
  • Nasal Endoscopy: We use a tiny camera tube to look deep inside your sinuses (it’s quick and generally painless).
  • CT Scan: For a detailed view of the size and location of the polyps.

Effective Treatment Options

Our goal is to reduce the size of the polyps or remove them completely. Here is how we treat them:

1. Medications

  • Nasal Sprays: Steroid sprays are often the first step to reduce swelling.
  • Oral Medication: Short courses of pills can help shrink polyps quickly.
  • Biologics: New, advanced injections for stubborn cases.

2. Saline Rinses

Washing your nose with a sterile saltwater solution helps clear mucus and irritants, helping you feel fresher immediately.

3. Surgery

If medicine doesn't work, we may suggest Endoscopic Sinus Surgery. This is a modern procedure available at Maria Hospital. It is usually done without any cuts on the outside of your face, allowing for a faster recovery.

When Should You See a Doctor?

Don't suffer in silence. You should book an appointment if symptoms last longer than 10 days.

Seek immediate help if you experience:

  • Sudden trouble breathing.
  • Double vision or vision changes.
  • Severe swelling around your eyes.
  • Severe headache with high fever.
