Nasal Allergies, Hay Fever and Allergic Rhinitis Treatment

Do you feel like you have a cold that just won’t go away? If you are dealing with constant sneezing, a runny nose, or itchy eyes, you might not be sick with a virus. You may have Allergic Rhinitis, also known as nasal allergies or hay fever.

What is Allergic Rhinitis?

Allergic rhinitis is inflammation in your nose. It happens when your immune system gets confused. It treats harmless things in the air—like dust, pollen, or pet fur—as dangerous invaders.

When you breathe these in, your body fights back by releasing chemicals (like histamine). This reaction causes the swelling, itching, and sneezing you feel. It is very common among both adults and children in Nepal.

Common Symptoms

The symptoms can start minutes after you breathe in dust or pollen. Look out for these signs:

  • Nasal Issues: Constant sneezing, a blocked nose, or a runny nose (usually clear liquid).
  • Itching: Itchy nose, throat, eyes, or even ears.
  • Eye Problems: Red, watery, or puffy eyes.
  • Fatigue: Feeling tired because the symptoms are ruining your sleep.
  • Physical Signs: Dark circles under the eyes or a line across the nose from rubbing it too much.

Is it a Cold or an Allergy?

It can be hard to tell the difference. Here is a quick checklist:

Allergies Common Cold
Discharge is thin and watery. Discharge is thick, yellow, or green.
You rarely have a fever. You often have a fever or body aches.
Itching is very common. Itching is rare.
Lasts as long as the dust/pollen is there. Usually goes away in 3 to 14 days.

Common Triggers in Nepal

Understanding what triggers your sneezing is key to a correct diagnosis.

1. Seasonal Allergies (Hay Fever)

These happen at specific times of the year when plants release pollen.

  • Spring: Tree pollen.
  • Summer: Grass pollen.
  • Autumn: Weed pollen.

2. Year-Round Allergies

These happen anytime and are caused by indoor triggers common in Nepali homes:

  • Dust Mites: Tiny bugs living in carpets and bedding.
  • Pet Dander: Tiny flakes of skin from dogs or cats.
  • Mold: Found in damp areas like bathrooms or ground-floor rooms.

Diagnosis and Health Checkup

If you are tired of buying medicine from the pharmacy without results, you need a proper doctor consultation. At Maria Hospital, we help identify the exact cause.

  • Physical Exam: A doctor will check your nose and throat for swelling.
  • Skin Prick Test: A quick, simple test to see if your skin reacts to specific allergens.
  • Blood Test: To measure how your immune system is responding.

Treatment Options

Once we know the cause, we can create a treatment plan for you.

  • Nasal Sprays: These reduce swelling and are often the most effective treatment.
  • Antihistamines: Pills or syrups that stop the itching and sneezing.
  • Saline Rinses: Washing your nose with salt water (similar to Jal Neti) to flush out dust and pollen.
  • Immunotherapy: If medicines don't work, allergy shots can help your body build resistance over time.

Prevention: Protect Yourself at Home

  • Manage Dust: Wash bedsheets weekly in hot water.
  • Close Windows: Keep windows shut during high pollen seasons or dusty days.
  • Avoid Mold: Fix leaks and keep bathrooms dry.
  • Wear a Mask: A good mask helps filter out dust and pollen when you are outside in Kathmandu.

When Should You Visit a Doctor?

You don't have to live with a blocked nose forever. You should book a health checkup if:

  • Your allergies are affecting your sleep, work, or studies.
  • Over-the-counter medicines are not helping.
  • You have asthma (allergies can make asthma worse).
  • You have sinus pain or a fever.
