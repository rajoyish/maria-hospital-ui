Sinusitis (Acute and Chronic) Treatment

Do you constantly feel like you have a heavy head or a blocked nose? You are not alone. Sinusitis, commonly known as a sinus infection, affects many people in Nepal every year, especially with the changing weather and pollution levels in cities like Kathmandu.

At Maria Hospital, we want to help you understand what is happening inside your body. This guide explains the symptoms of sinusitis and how to get the right diagnosis and treatment to feel better fast.

What is Sinusitis?

Your sinuses are hollow, air-filled spaces behind your forehead, cheekbones, and nose. When these spaces are healthy, air flows through them easily. However, when the tissues lining them become swollen or blocked with fluid, germs can grow and cause an infection.

Doctors generally divide sinusitis into three types based on how long it lasts:

  • Acute Sinusitis: Usually starts like a common cold and lasts less than 4 weeks. Most of these are caused by viruses and go away on their own.
  • Chronic Sinusitis: A long-term condition where symptoms last for more than 12 weeks. This is often caused by long-lasting inflammation (swelling).
  • Recurrent Acute Sinusitis: When you get a sinus infection four or more times in a single year.

Common Symptoms: The "Three Ps"

Sinusitis can make you feel tired and miserable. To spot it easily, remember the "Three Ps":

  1. Pus: Thick yellow or green mucus coming from your nose or dripping down your throat.
  2. Pressure: Pain or tenderness around your eyes, cheeks, and forehead (often worse when you bend down).
  3. Plugged: A blocked or stuffy nose that makes breathing difficult.

Other signs to watch for include:

  • Reduced sense of smell or taste.
  • Fever and bad breath.
  • Upper tooth pain.
  • Coughing (which may get worse at night).

Causes and Risk Factors

While a viral cold is the most common cause, other factors can lead to sinus problems. In the dusty environment of Kathmandu, Nepal, these triggers are very common:

  • Allergies: Dust, pollen, and pollution can trigger swelling.
  • Nasal Polyps: Small growths inside the nose that block airflow.
  • Deviated Septum: A crooked wall between your nostrils that causes blockage.
  • Smoking: Smoke irritates the nose and increases infection risk.

Effective Treatment Options

The right treatment depends on whether your infection is new (acute) or long-term (chronic).

1. Home Remedies and Self-Care

For mild cases, you can often treat symptoms at home:

  • Saline Irrigation (Jala Neti): Rinse your nasal passages with a neti pot or saline spray to clear out dust and mucus.
  • Steam Inhalation: Breathe in steam from a bowl of hot water to open your blocked nose.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of warm water and soups.
  • Warm Compresses: Put a warm, damp towel on your face to reduce pain.

2. Medical Treatment

If home remedies don't help, a doctor consultation is necessary. Treatments may include:

  • Decongestants: To briefly help with a blocked nose (do not use for more than 3-5 days).
  • Nasal Sprays: Steroid sprays to reduce swelling.
  • Antibiotics: These are only needed if bacteria are causing the infection. Your doctor will decide if you need them based on a proper health checkup.

3. Chronic Care

If your sinusitis lasts for months, you may need to see an ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) specialist at Maria Hospital. We offer advanced care, including procedures to open sinus pathways if medication alone does not work.

When to Visit Maria Hospital

Most sinus infections heal with time, but some require professional medical attention. You should book a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital if:

  • Your symptoms last longer than 10 days.
  • You start feeling better, but then get suddenly worse ("double sickening").
  • You have a high fever, severe headache, or swelling around your eyes.

Prevention Tips

  • Wash your hands often to avoid colds.
  • Wear a mask in dusty areas to manage allergies.
  • Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke.
