Swollen Lymph Nodes in the Neck Treatment

Finding a lump in your neck can feel scary, but most of the time, it simply means your body is doing its job. Lymph nodes are tiny, bean-shaped filters in your immune system that trap and destroy viruses and bacteria. You have hundreds of them throughout your body, with many located around your neck, jaw, and ears.

When these nodes swell, it usually means you are actively fighting off a common illness. If you are looking for a reliable health checkup or doctor consultation in Kathmandu, Nepal, Maria Hospital is here to help you understand your symptoms, find the cause, and get the right care.

What Causes Neck Lumps?

Most swollen lymph nodes are harmless and go away on their own. The most common causes include:

  • Common Infections: Colds, the flu, ear infections, throat infections, or dental issues are the most frequent culprits behind swollen nodes.
  • Autoimmune Conditions: Sometimes, conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy cells can cause swelling.
  • Medications: In rare cases, certain prescription medicines can cause swollen nodes as a side effect.
  • Serious Conditions: While uncommon, a lump can occasionally be a sign of a more serious illness, like cancer. These lumps usually feel hard, do not hurt, and do not move when you press on them.

When to Seek a Doctor Consultation

Usually, a swollen gland caused by a standard infection will shrink back to normal within 1 to 2 weeks. However, you should visit Maria Hospital for a proper diagnosis if you notice any of these warning signs:

  • The lump lasts for more than 2 to 4 weeks or keeps growing.
  • It feels hard, rubbery, or stuck in place under your skin.
  • You experience sudden weight loss, heavy night sweats, or an unexplained fever.
  • The lump is larger than 1.5 centimeters.
  • You have trouble swallowing, breathing, or speaking.

What to Expect During Your Checkup at Maria Hospital

When you visit our ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) specialists in Kathmandu, our primary goal is to find the exact cause of your swelling so we can start the right treatment. Your visit will typically include:

  1. Discussing Your Health: We will listen to your symptoms and ask about your medical history and lifestyle.
  2. A Gentle Physical Exam: The doctor will feel your neck and carefully check your ears, nose, and throat for any signs of infection.
  3. A Quick Scope Check: We might use a tiny, flexible camera (endoscope) to safely and comfortably look at the back of your throat while you are awake.
  4. Further Testing: If the cause isn't immediately clear, we may recommend a simple blood test, a quick and painless ultrasound, or a small tissue sample (biopsy) for lab testing.

Your Treatment Plan

Your treatment will depend entirely on what is causing the lump:

  • For Viruses: Antibiotics do not cure viral infections. We usually suggest rest, warm compresses, drinking plenty of fluids, and taking basic pain relievers while your body heals naturally.
  • For Bacteria: Your doctor will likely prescribe a short course of antibiotics to clear up the infection.
  • For Serious Conditions: If further medical care is needed, our expert team will guide you through a specialized, step-by-step treatment plan tailored to your needs.
