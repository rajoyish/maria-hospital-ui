Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS) Treatment

Do you often feel like one side of your nose is blocked? Do you struggle with snoring or frequent sinus infections? You might have a condition called Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS). While this sounds complex, it is actually very common.

At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, we are here to help you understand this condition and find the right treatment so you can breathe freely again.

What is the Nasal Septum?

Think of your nose as a house with two rooms. The "nasal septum" is the wall that divides these two rooms (your nostrils). It is made of bone and cartilage. Ideally, this wall should be straight, making both sides of your nose the same size.

What is a Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS)?

A "deviated" septum simply means the wall is crooked or bent to one side. This makes one nasal passage smaller than the other, which can block airflow and make breathing difficult.

Did you know? It is very rare to have a perfectly straight nose. About 80% of people have some bend in their septum. For many, it causes no issues. However, if the bend is severe, it requires medical attention.

Why Does It Happen?

There are a few reasons why your septum might be crooked:

  • Born with it: Some babies are born with a deviated septum due to the way the nose developed in the womb.
  • Injury or Trauma: A hit to the nose from sports, a fall, or a traffic accident is a common cause.
  • Aging: As we get older, our nasal structures change, which can make a slight bend worse over time.

Common Signs and Symptoms

How do you know if you need a doctor consultation? Look out for these symptoms:

  • Blocked Nose: Difficulty breathing through one or both nostrils.
  • Nosebleeds: The bent area can become dry and crack easily.
  • Snoring & Noisy Breathing: A narrowed airway often leads to loud snoring or sleep issues.
  • Headaches: Pressure inside the nose can cause facial pain.
  • Recurring Sinus Infections: If your nose cannot drain properly, you may get sick often.

Diagnosis and Treatment at Maria Hospital

If you are suffering from these symptoms, it is time for a health checkup at Maria Hospital. Ignoring the problem can lead to chronic sleep issues and sinus trouble.

How we check you:

Our specialists in Kathmandu use safe, modern methods to provide an accurate diagnosis:

  • Physical Exam: We gently look inside your nose.
  • Nasal Endoscopy: We use a tiny camera to see deep inside your nasal passages.
  • CT Scan: If needed, we take detailed images of your nasal bones.

How we treat you:

Your treatment depends on how much the condition bothers you.

  1. Medicine (Non-Surgical): For mild cases, we may prescribe sprays or decongestants to reduce swelling and help you breathe.
  2. Septoplasty (Surgery): If medicine doesn't help, we may suggest a minor surgery called Septoplasty. This procedure straightens the wall inside your nose. It is done entirely inside the nostrils, so there are no visible scars on your face.
