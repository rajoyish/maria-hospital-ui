Here is everything you need to know about ear wax, symptoms of blockage, and safe treatment options.

What is Ear Wax and Why Do We Have It?

Ear wax (medically called cerumen) is actually a sign of a healthy ear. Your body produces it to:

  • Trap dust and dirt.
  • Keep water out of the ear canal.
  • Protect your sensitive eardrum from infection.

Usually, your ears are "self-cleaning." When you chew or talk, the movement of your jaw slowly pushes old wax out of the ear opening, where it dries and falls out. However, sometimes this process gets stuck, leading to a blockage.

Signs You Might Have an Ear Wax Blockage

How do you know if you need a doctor consultation? Look out for these common symptoms:

  • Hearing Loss: Sounds seem quiet or muffled.
  • Fullness: A feeling that your ear is plugged up (like being underwater).
  • Earache: Pain or discomfort inside the ear.
  • Tinnitus: Ringing, buzzing, or humming noises.
  • Itching: Constant itchiness deep inside the ear.
  • Dizziness: Feeling off-balance.

Why Does Wax Build Up?

Blockages can happen to anyone, but here are the most common reasons:

  1. Cotton Swabs (Q-Tips): This is the #1 cause. Using cotton buds often pushes wax deeper into the ear instead of removing it.
  2. Earphones and Hearing Aids: Frequent use stops wax from falling out naturally.
  3. Anatomy: Some people just have narrower ear canals.
  4. Age: As we get older, ear wax becomes harder and drier.

How to Treat Ear Wax Blockage Safely

1. Safe Home Remedies

If your ear hurts or you have a history of ear holes (perforations), do not try this at home. Visit Maria Hospital instead.

If your ear is healthy otherwise, you can try to soften the wax:

  • Use Drops: Put a few drops of baby oil, mineral oil, or glycerin in your ear.
  • Wait: Let it sit for 15 to 30 minutes to soften the hard wax.
  • Gentle Rinse: You can gently rinse the ear with warm water (body temperature) using a bulb syringe. Tilt your head to let the water run out.

2. Professional Medical Treatment

If home remedies don't work, it is time for a professional health checkup. At Maria Hospital, our doctors use safe, specialized tools for diagnosis and removal:

  • Irrigation: Flushing the ear safely with water.
  • Manual Removal: Using small medical tools to carefully pull the wax out.
  • Microsuction: A gentle vacuum technique often used by ENT specialists to suck the wax out without water.

⚠️ Important: What NOT To Do

To protect your hearing, please follow these safety rules:

  • NO Cotton Swabs: Never put cotton buds, pins, or matchsticks inside your ear. You risk puncturing your eardrum.
  • NO Ear Candles: These are dangerous and can cause burns or serious damage.
  • NO High Pressure: Do not use water jets (like dental flossers) in your ear.

When Should You See a Doctor?

Skip the home remedies and seek a diagnosis immediately if:

  • You have severe ear pain or bleeding.
  • There is fluid or a bad smell coming from your ear.
  • You have sudden hearing loss.
  • You have diabetes or a weakened immune system.
