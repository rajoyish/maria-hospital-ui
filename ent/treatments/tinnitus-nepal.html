Tinnitus Treatment

Do you hear a buzzing, humming, or ringing sound in your ears, even when the room is completely quiet? You are not alone. This condition is called Tinnitus (pronounced tin-NITE-us), and it is a very common issue for many people in Nepal.

At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, we understand how frustrating these sounds can be. The good news is that tinnitus is usually not a disease itself. Instead, it is a symptom of other conditions, like hearing loss or simple ear blockages. You do not have to suffer in silence—help is available.

What Does Tinnitus Sound Like?

While people often call it "ringing in the ears," tinnitus can sound different for everyone. You might hear:

  • Buzzing or humming (like an electric wire).
  • Hissing, clicking, or whistling.
  • Roaring (like the sound of the ocean).
  • Pulsing (a thumping sound that beats in time with your heart).

These sounds might happen in one ear, both ears, or feel like they are inside your head. They might come and go, or stay with you all day.

Why Is This Happening? (Common Causes)

Doctors believe tinnitus is often the brain’s reaction to changes in the ear. To understand this, it helps to look at how the ear is structured.

Common triggers include:

  • Loud Noise: Exposure to loud traffic in Kathmandu, construction noise, or loud music can damage delicate parts of the inner ear.
  • Ear Blockages: A simple buildup of earwax or dirt can block your ear canal.
  • Aging: As we get older (usually over 60), our hearing naturally changes.
  • Medications: Certain antibiotics or painkillers can trigger these sounds.
  • Health Conditions: High blood pressure, diabetes, or jaw problems can also be linked to tinnitus.

When Should I See a Doctor?

For some, tinnitus is just a small annoyance. However, you should book a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital if:

  • The noise has lasted for more than one week.
  • The sound affects your sleep, focus, or makes you feel anxious.
  • The noise is significantly bothering you in your daily life.

⚠️ Seek immediate medical attention if:

  • You have sudden hearing loss along with the ringing.
  • You feel dizziness or vertigo (the room feels like it is spinning).
  • You hear a pulsing sound that matches your heartbeat.

How We Diagnose and Treat Tinnitus

When you visit Maria Hospital for a health checkup, our team will review your history and examine your ears, head, and neck. We may perform:

  • Hearing Exams: To check how well you hear different sounds.
  • Movement Tests: Checking if moving your jaw or eyes changes the sound.
  • Lab Tests: Simple blood tests or scans to rule out other issues.

Effective Management Strategies

While there is no single "magic pill" for tinnitus, treating the root cause often stops the noise.

  • Earwax Removal: If wax is the cause, our doctors can safely remove it.
  • Medication Adjustment: If a medicine is causing the ringing, we can help you find an alternative.
  • Sound Therapy: Using "white noise" (like the sound of a fan or rain) can cover up the ringing so you can sleep better.
  • Lifestyle Changes: Reducing stress, avoiding loud noises, and lowering caffeine intake can help reduce symptoms.
