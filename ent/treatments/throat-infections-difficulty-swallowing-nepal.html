Throat Infections and Difficulty Swallowing Treatment

Swallowing is something we do hundreds of times a day without thinking. But when it becomes painful or difficult, it can feel scary and uncomfortable. At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, our goal is to help you understand why this happens, how to spot a serious infection, and when it is time to seek a doctor consultation for proper care.

Why Does Swallowing Hurt?

Doctors use two main terms to describe these uncomfortable symptoms:

Dysphagia
The feeling that food or liquid is stuck in your throat or chest, or that it takes extra effort to swallow.
Odynophagia
Sharp pain when swallowing that happens in your mouth, throat, or food pipe.

While everyday issues like acid reflux can cause discomfort, sudden pain and swallowing trouble are very often caused by a throat infection.

Common Throat Infections That Affect Swallowing

If your throat hurts when you swallow, inflammation is likely the cause. Here are the most common reasons you might be feeling pain:

1. Viral Infections (Colds and Flu)

Viruses are the leading cause of a sore throat. If you also have a runny nose, cough, and are sneezing, a viral infection is likely to blame. These usually clear up on their own within a week.

2. Strep Throat

This is a bacterial infection that requires a professional diagnosis and medical treatment. Unlike a common cold, strep throat usually does not cause a cough or runny nose.

Watch out for these signs:

  • Sudden, severe sore throat
  • Fever
  • Swollen, tender lymph nodes in your neck
  • White patches or streaks of pus on your tonsils
  • Tiny red spots on the roof of your mouth

3. Tonsillitis

This is the painful swelling of your tonsils (the two soft pads at the back of your throat). Caused by either viruses or bacteria, symptoms include red tonsils, bad breath, fever, and sharp pain when you swallow.

4. Fungal Infections (Thrush)

Sometimes, a yeast infection called oral thrush can develop in the throat, especially for people with weakened immune systems. Symptoms include a cotton-like feeling in your mouth, loss of taste, and white patches on your tongue or inner cheeks.

5. Epiglottitis (A Medical Emergency)

This is a rare but highly dangerous infection that affects the flap protecting your windpipe, potentially blocking your airway. Symptoms include a sudden, severe sore throat, high fever, a muffled voice, and drooling because swallowing saliva is too painful. Seek emergency care immediately if you notice these signs.

Other Reasons You Might Struggle to Swallow

If you do not have a fever or feel sick, a health checkup might reveal other common causes, such as:

  • Acid Reflux (GERD): Stomach acid backing up can irritate your throat and make it feel like there is a lump in it.
  • Throat Injury: Scratching your throat on sharp or very hot food.
  • Allergies: Swelling or post-nasal drip from allergic reactions can severely irritate the throat.

Simple Home Remedies to Soothe Your Throat

If your swallowing difficulty is caused by a mild cold, these home care tips can help you feel better:

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of fluids. Warm liquids like broth or tea with honey can soothe the throat, while cool drinks can help numb the pain.
  • Salt Water Gargle: Mix half a teaspoon of salt into a glass of warm water and gargle to reduce swelling and clear away bacteria.
  • Humidify the Air: Use a humidifier or breathe in steam to keep your throat from drying out.
  • Pain Relievers: Over-the-counter medicines like paracetamol or ibuprofen can help reduce fever and pain.

    Note: Never give aspirin to children or teenagers.
