Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) Treatment

As men get older, body changes are natural. For many men over 50 in Nepal, one of the most common changes involves the prostate gland. This condition is called Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH).

While the medical name sounds complicated, it simply means you have an enlarged prostate. The most important thing to know is that BPH is not cancer. However, it can cause uncomfortable symptoms that disrupt your daily routine.

If you are worried about your symptoms, a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital can give you the answers you need.

What is the Prostate?

The prostate is a small gland, about the size of a walnut. It is found just below the bladder and wraps around the tube that carries urine out of the body (the urethra).

When the prostate grows too large, it squeezes this tube. Imagine stepping on a garden hoseâ€”the water flows slowly or stops. This is exactly what happens to your urine flow when you have BPH.

Is BPH the Same as Prostate Cancer?

No. BPH is benign, which means it is non-cancerous. Having an enlarged prostate does not increase your risk of getting prostate cancer.

However, because BPH and prostate cancer can have similar warning signs, you should never ignore them. A proper diagnosis by a specialist is the only way to be sure.

Who is at Risk?

BPH is a normal part of aging for most men.

  • Age: By age 60, half of all men will notice signs. By age 85, nearly 90% of men are affected.
  • Family History: If your father or brother had prostate issues, you might too.
  • Lifestyle: Factors like weight, heart health, and diabetes can play a role.

Common Symptoms to Watch For

Symptoms can be different for every man. You might have a very large prostate but few symptoms, or a slightly enlarged one with major issues. Look out for these "Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms":

  • Frequent Urination: Needing to visit the toilet often, especially waking up multiple times at night.
  • Urgency: A sudden need to pee that is hard to control.
  • Weak Stream: Urine flow that is slow or stops and starts.
  • Straining: Having to push hard to start urinating.
  • Incomplete Emptying: Feeling like your bladder is still full after you finish.

Emergency Note: If you cannot pass any urine at all, this is an emergency called urinary retention. Please seek immediate medical help.

Treatment Options at Maria Hospital

You do not have to live with these symptoms. There are many effective ways to treat BPH, ranging from simple habit changes to advanced medical procedures. Your specific treatment plan will depend on your health and the size of your prostate.

1. Lifestyle Changes

If your symptoms are mild, your doctor might suggest "watchful waiting." Simple changes can help:

  • Drink less coffee and alcohol.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Avoid drinking liquids right before bed.

2. Medications

For moderate symptoms, medicine is often the first step.

  • Relaxers (Alpha-blockers): These help relax muscles in the prostate to make peeing easier.
  • Shrinkers (5-Alpha Reductase Inhibitors): These help stop the prostate from growing or make it smaller.

3. Minimally Invasive & Surgical Procedures

If medicine is not enough, Maria Hospital offers modern surgical solutions. We prioritize techniques that offer quick recovery and safety.

  • TURP (The Gold Standard): This is the most common surgery where the inner part of the prostate is trimmed away to restore urine flow. Modern bipolar TURP is safer and very effective.
  • Laser Surgery: Uses high-energy lasers to vaporize excess tissue with less bleeding and faster healing.
  • Minimally Invasive Therapies: Options like water vapor therapy or prostate lifts can open the urinary pathway without major surgery.
  • Open Surgery: Reserved for very large prostates to remove the obstruction completely.
