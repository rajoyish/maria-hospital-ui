Gonorrhea Treatment

Gonorrhea is a very common sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by bacteria. In Nepal, many people may feel shy talking about sexual health, but understanding this infection is vital for your well-being. It spreads easily and can infect both men and women.

The good news is that Gonorrhea is fully curable. However, if left untreated, it can cause permanent health problems. If you are worried about your sexual health, a private doctor consultation at Maria Hospital can provide the answers you need.

How Do You Get Gonorrhea?

Gonorrhea is spread through sexual contact. You can get the infection by having vaginal, anal, or oral sex without protection (like a condom).

  • Sexual Fluids: The bacteria live in fluids like semen and vaginal discharge.
  • Mother to Baby: A pregnant woman can pass the infection to her newborn during childbirth.
  • What does NOT spread it: You cannot get Gonorrhea from hugging, kissing, sharing food, or sitting on toilet seats.

The "Silent" Infection: Watch for These Symptoms

Many people—especially women—do not show any symptoms at all. You can look healthy and still pass the infection to others. If symptoms do appear, they usually start within two weeks.

Symptoms in Women

Symptoms can often feel like a normal bladder infection. Watch out for:

  • Pain or burning when urinating (peeing).
  • Unusual vaginal discharge (white, yellow, or greenish).
  • Vaginal bleeding between periods.
  • Pain in the lower stomach or during sex.

Symptoms in Men

Men are more likely to notice signs, including:

  • A burning feeling when urinating.
  • White, yellow, or green discharge (pus) from the penis.
  • Painful or swollen testicles.

Symptoms in Other Body Parts

  • Rectum (Anus): Itching, discharge, or pain.
  • Throat: Sore throat or swollen glands.
  • Eyes: Redness, pain, or sensitivity to light.

Diagnosis: How to Know for Sure

You cannot guess if you have Gonorrhea based on feelings alone. The only way to know is to get a professional health checkup.

At Maria Hospital, Kathmandu in Nepal, we offer confidential testing methods:

  • Urine Test: A simple sample of your urine is checked for bacteria.
  • Swab Test: A doctor gently takes a sample from the infected area (like the throat or cervix).

Who should get tested?

If you are sexually active, we recommend a regular checkup, especially if you have a new partner or multiple partners.

Treatment and Cure

Yes, Gonorrhea is curable! Because the bacteria are strong, you need the right medical treatment.

  • Antibiotics: Your doctor will usually give you an injection (shot) and sometimes oral pills.
  • Treating Partners: Your sexual partners from the last 60 days must also get tested and treated to stop the infection from coming back.
  • Wait to have sex: You should avoid sexual contact for seven days after finishing your medication.

Important: Always finish all your medicine, even if you feel better. This ensures the infection is completely gone.

Why You Should Not Ignore It

If you do not get a diagnosis and treatment, Gonorrhea can cause serious damage to your body:

  • Infertility: In both men and women, untreated Gonorrhea can damage the reproductive organs, making it difficult or impossible to have children later.
  • PID: Women can develop Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID), which is painful and dangerous.
  • Pregnancy Risks: Mothers can pass the infection to babies, leading to blindness or severe blood infections in the newborn.
  • HIV Risk: Having untreated Gonorrhea increases your risk of getting HIV.

Prevention: Protect Yourself

The best way to stay safe is to practice safe sex:

  • Use Condoms: Use them correctly every time you have sex.
  • Limit Partners: Having fewer sexual partners reduces your risk.
  • Get Tested: Regular screenings are part of a healthy lifestyle.
