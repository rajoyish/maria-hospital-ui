Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) Treatment

Are you experiencing unexplained pelvic pain or unusual discomfort? You might be dealing with Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID). While this condition is common, many women in Nepal delay seeking help because they are unsure of the symptoms.

At Maria Hospital, we believe that understanding your body is the first step to good health. This article will help you understand PID, why early diagnosis matters, and how to get effective treatment in Kathmandu.

What is Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)?

Pelvic Inflammatory Disease, or PID, is an infection of a woman's reproductive organs. It affects the upper genital tract, which includes:

  • The uterus (womb)
  • The fallopian tubes
  • The ovaries

The infection usually happens when bacteria move up from the vagina or cervix into these internal organs. The good news is that if you book a doctor consultation early, PID is completely curable. However, if left untreated, it can lead to long-term health issues, including difficulty getting pregnant.

What Causes PID?

PID is caused by bacteria. In many cases, it is linked to untreated sexually transmitted infections (STIs) like chlamydia and gonorrhea.

However, it is important to know that not all cases are caused by STIs. You can also develop PID if normal bacteria travel up into your reproductive organs. This can happen after:

  • Childbirth
  • A miscarriage or abortion
  • Pelvic surgery
  • Getting an IUD (Intrauterine Device) inserted

Common Symptoms: What to Look For

PID can be tricky because symptoms are often mild. Some women feel no pain at all. However, if you notice any of the following, it is time for a health checkup:

  • Lower Abdominal Pain: Pain or tenderness in your lower belly or pelvis is the most common sign.
  • Unusual Discharge: Increased vaginal discharge that has a bad smell or looks yellow/green.
  • Pain During Sex: Discomfort deep inside during intercourse.
  • Urinary Issues: A burning sensation or pain when you pass urine.
  • Irregular Bleeding: Spotting between periods or bleeding after sex.
  • Flu-like Symptoms: Fever, chills, nausea, or vomiting.

Note: If you have severe pain, high fever, or cannot keep food down, please seek urgent medical care immediately.

Diagnosis and Doctor Consultation

How do we confirm if you have PID? At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, our compassionate doctors use a combination of methods to give you an accurate diagnosis:

  • Medical History: We will chat about your symptoms and health history in a confidential setting.
  • Pelvic Exam: The doctor will gently check for tenderness in your pelvic area.
  • Lab Tests: We may test fluid samples or urine to identify the bacteria causing the infection.
  • Ultrasound: In some cases, we use ultrasound imaging to get a clear picture of your reproductive organs.

Treatment Options in Nepal

Once diagnosed, PID is generally treated with antibiotics. Since different bacteria can cause the infection, your doctor at Maria Hospital may prescribe a combination of medicines to ensure the infection is fully cleared.

For successful treatment, remember to:

  • Finish Your Medicine: Take every pill prescribed, even if you start feeling better.
  • Treat Your Partner: If the cause is an STI, your partner must also get a health checkup and treatment to prevent reinfection.
  • Avoid Sex: Wait until treatment is finished and symptoms are gone.
  • Follow Up: Visit us again within 2-3 days to ensure the medicine is working.

Why Early Treatment Matters

Treating PID quickly is vital. Delaying a doctor consultation can lead to serious complications, such as:

  • Infertility: Blocked fallopian tubes can make it difficult to get pregnant.
  • Ectopic Pregnancy: A dangerous condition where a fertilized egg grows outside the uterus.
  • Chronic Pain: Long-lasting pelvic pain that can persist for months.
