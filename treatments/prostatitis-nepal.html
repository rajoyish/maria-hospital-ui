What is Prostatitis?

The prostate is a small, walnut-sized gland located just below your bladder. When it becomes inflamed or swollen, it is called Prostatitis.

Doctors usually classify it into four types:

Acute Bacterial Prostatitis
A sudden bacterial infection with severe symptoms.
Chronic Bacterial Prostatitis
An infection that keeps coming back over time.
Chronic Pelvic Pain Syndrome (CPPS)
The most common type. It causes long-term pain in the pelvic area, even without an infection.
Asymptomatic
You have inflammation but feel no pain. This is usually found during a routine health checkup.

Common Signs and Symptoms

Symptoms can look different for every man. However, if you notice these changes, it is time for a diagnosis and checkup:

1. Urinary Issues

  • A burning sensation or pain when passing urine.
  • Needing to urinate often, especially at night.
  • Trouble starting to pee or a weak stream (dribbling).
  • Cloudy or bloody urine.

2. Pain and Discomfort

  • Pain in the lower back, groin, or stomach area.
  • Discomfort in the testicles or penis.
  • Pain during ejaculation.

⚠️ Emergency Warning: If you have a sudden fever, chills, body aches, and cannot urinate at all, seek immediate medical help. This could be a severe infection.

What Causes It?

The cause depends on the type of prostatitis you have:

  • Bacteria: Common bacteria (like E. coli) can enter the prostate from the urinary tract.
  • Stress & Tension: Emotional stress or tight muscles in the pelvic floor can trigger pain.
  • Lifestyle: Spicy food, alcohol, or injury can sometimes irritate the area.

Effective Treatment Options

The good news is that Prostatitis is treatable. Your treatment plan will depend on your specific condition:

  • Medication: Antibiotics are very effective for bacterial infections. Alpha-blockers can help relax muscles to make urinating easier.
  • Pain Relief: Simple medicines (like ibuprofen) can reduce swelling and pain.
  • Physical Therapy: Specialized massage or exercises can relax tight pelvic muscles.
  • Lifestyle Changes:
    • Diet: Avoid too much alcohol, caffeine, and very spicy foods.
    • Hydration: Drink plenty of water to flush out your system.
    • Comfort: If you sit for long periods (like office work or driving), use a cushion to reduce pressure.
