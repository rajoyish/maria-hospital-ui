Trichomoniasis Treatment

At Maria Hospital, we believe that good health starts with the right information. One of the most common health concerns we see is Trichomoniasis (often called "trich").

If you are worried about your sexual health, you are not alone. This guide explains what you need to know about this common infection and how you can get a quick diagnosis and treatment right here in Kathmandu, Nepal.

What is Trichomoniasis?

Trichomoniasis is a very common Sexually Transmitted Infection (STI). It is caused by a tiny parasite. The most important thing to remember is that it is completely curable.

While it is easily treated with medication, if you ignore it, the infection can last for months or even years.

How Does it Spread?

The parasite passes from one person to another during sexual contact.

  • How you get it: Through vaginal, anal, or oral sex, or close skin-to-skin genital contact.
  • How you do NOT get it: You cannot catch it from hugging, kissing, sharing plates, or sitting on toilet seats.

Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

This infection is tricky because most people (about 70%) do not have any symptoms. You might have the infection and feel perfectly healthy.

If symptoms do appear, they usually show up 5 to 28 days after exposure.

Symptoms in Women:

  • Discharge: Thin or frothy discharge that may be clear, white, yellow, or green, often with a bad smell.
  • Discomfort: Itching, burning, redness, or soreness in the private area.
  • Pain: Pain while urinating or during sex.

Symptoms in Men:

  • Itching or irritation inside the penis.
  • Burning sensation after urination or ejaculation.
  • Unusual discharge from the penis.

Note: If you notice any of these signs, we recommend booking a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital immediately.

Why You Should Get Treated

Even if you feel fine, untreated Trichomoniasis can lead to serious health issues:

  • Pregnancy Risks: It can cause babies to be born too early or with low birth weight.
  • Higher HIV Risk: It causes inflammation that makes it easier to catch HIV or pass it to others.
  • Infertility: Without treatment, it can lead to complications that make it harder to have children later.

Diagnosis and Treatment at Maria Hospital

You cannot diagnose this infection just by guessing. A proper health checkup is necessary.

1. The Test

Our lab in Kathmandu uses accurate testing methods. A doctor will take a fluid sample (using a swab) or a urine sample. It is quick, private, and painless.

2. The Cure

The treatment is simple: oral antibiotics prescribed by a doctor.

  • Medication: Usually a 7-day course of pills for women, or a single dose for men.
  • Treat Partners: Your partner must get treated at the same time, or you will likely pass the infection back and forth.
  • Wait: Avoid sex for about one week until the medicine is finished and symptoms are gone.

Important Warning: Do not drink alcohol while taking this medication and for at least 24 hours after finishing it. Mixing alcohol with these antibiotics can cause severe vomiting.

Prevention and Emotional Health

We understand that an STI diagnosis can be stressful. Please remember:

  • It is common: Many people in Nepal have it.
  • It does not prove cheating: You or your partner could have carried the infection for a long time without knowing.
  • Prevention: Using condoms lowers your risk significantly.
