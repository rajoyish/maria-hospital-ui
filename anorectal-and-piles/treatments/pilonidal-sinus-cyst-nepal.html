Pilonidal Sinus (Cyst) Treatment

Dealing with unexplained pain near your tailbone can be worrying, but you are not alone. A pilonidal sinus (sometimes called a pilonidal cyst) is a small tunnel or pocket in the skin, usually found at the very top of the buttocks. These pockets often trap loose hair, dirt, and dead skin cells.

While they can stay quiet and painless for a long time, they can easily become infected, leading to a painful, swollen lump filled with pus. At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, our experienced medical team is here to provide gentle, effective care to help you find lasting relief.

What Causes It?

Think of it like getting a splinter. Loose body hairs can be pushed back into the skin due to friction from tight clothes, walking, or sitting for long hours. Your body treats this trapped hair as a foreign object and reacts by forming a cyst around it to protect you.

Who is Most at Risk?

Anyone can develop this condition, but certain factors make it more common:

  • Gender: Men get it more often because they tend to have thicker body hair.
  • Age: It is most frequently seen in young adults, from teenagers to those in their early thirties.
  • Lifestyle: People with desk jobs or those who drive for long stretches face continuous pressure on the tailbone area.
  • Hair Type: Having thick, coarse, or excessive body hair increases your risk.
  • Weight & Hygiene: Being overweight or sweating heavily can create a moist environment where infections thrive.

Watch Out for These Symptoms

At first, you might only notice a small, painless dimple. However, if the pocket becomes infected, you should schedule a doctor consultation right away. Look out for:

  • Pain and tenderness that gets worse when you sit or stand.
  • A red, swollen, and warm lump near your tailbone.
  • Pus or blood draining from the area, often with an unpleasant smell.
  • Visible hair sticking out from the sore.
  • Fever, nausea, or feeling unusually tired.

  • Medication: Antibiotics may be prescribed alongside treatment to help clear up a spreading infection.

Simple Steps for Prevention

After your treatment, taking good care of the area is vital to stop the cyst from coming back:

  • Keep your lower back and tailbone area clean and completely dry.
  • Remove hair regularly from your lower back and buttocks using shaving, creams, or laser hair removal.
  • Take frequent breaks to stand up, stretch, and walk around if you sit for long hours.
  • Wear loose, breathable cotton clothes to reduce sweat and friction.
  • Maintain a healthy weight to relieve pressure on your lower back.
