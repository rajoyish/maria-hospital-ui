Labiaplasty

Every woman's body is completely unique, and that includes the labia. It is perfectly normal for the lips surrounding the vaginal opening to vary widely in shape, size, and color. But if you have enlarged or uneven labia, you might be dealing with physical discomfort or feeling a bit self-conscious.

What exactly is a labiaplasty?

Labiaplasty, often called labia reduction, is a straightforward surgery that reshapes or reduces the size of the inner or outer vaginal lips. The main goal of this treatment is to safely remove any excess tissue so the inner labia no longer hang uncomfortably past the outer labia.

Why do women choose to have this treatment?

Women decide to have a labiaplasty for all sorts of physical and emotional reasons. Things like natural aging, childbirth, hormonal shifts, and even just genetics can change your body over time. A few of the most common reasons women look into this procedure include:

  • Finding relief from physical pain: Extra tissue can easily twist, pinch, or chafe. This surgery can stop the pain you might feel while exercising, wearing tight clothes, or during intimacy.
  • Improving hygiene and health: Excess skin folds can trap moisture and bacteria. This sometimes leads to frustrating issues like chronic yeast or urinary tract infections. Reducing this tissue makes keeping things clean much easier.
  • Boosting self-esteem: It is common to feel self-conscious if your labia are uneven, dark, or larger than you would like. This procedure can be a wonderful way to improve your body image and give you peace of mind.

Expert diagnosis and the techniques we use

Your journey at Maria Hospital starts with a private, comfortable doctor consultation. Our highly trained surgeons will give you a thorough diagnosis to understand your unique anatomy and tailor the procedure exactly to your goals.

Here are the most common techniques we use:

  • The Trim Technique: Your surgeon carefully removes the extra tissue along the outer edge. This works incredibly well for reducing size, evening things out, and removing darker edges.
  • The Wedge Technique: The surgeon takes a small, V-shaped wedge from the thickest part of the labia and gently brings the edges together. This keeps your natural edge and color intact, leaving a hidden scar.
  • Clitoral Hood Reduction: Sometimes reducing just the labia can make the connected clitoral hood look a little bulky. To give you a balanced and natural look, our surgeons can gently remove a bit of the excess skin folds there too, without changing your sensation at all.

What to expect: Before, during, and after your treatment

Before your surgery

During your consultation, your doctor will go over your medical history and talk about what you hope to achieve. To make sure your surgery goes smoothly and you heal quickly, we will ask you to stop smoking and avoid blood-thinning medicines like ibuprofen for a few weeks beforehand.

During the procedure

Labiaplasty is an outpatient procedure. This means you get to go home and rest in your own bed the very same day.

  • The whole surgery usually takes about one to two hours.
  • We perform it safely using either local anesthesia so you are completely numb and relaxed, or general anesthesia where you sleep through it.
  • We use dissolvable stitches, so you will not have to worry about the discomfort of having them removed later.

Recovery and aftercare at home

Taking good care of yourself at home is the secret to the best results:

  • The first few days: It is totally normal to have some mild swelling, soreness, and bruising. Just rest, keep your pelvis elevated, and use ice packs over your underwear to keep the swelling down.
  • Daily hygiene: Keep the area clean by gently using a squirt bottle of lukewarm water after you use the bathroom. Pat the area dry very gently, and make sure never to rub it.
  • Getting back to your routine: Plan to take about a week off from work to recover. Stick to loose clothing and breathable cotton underwear.
  • Activity limits: To keep your stitches safe, you will need to completely avoid strenuous workouts, baths, tampons, and intimacy for about four to six weeks. You will see your beautiful final results settle in within three to six months.
