Heavy Periods (Menorrhagia) Treatment

Many women experience heavy menstrual bleeding, but it can be difficult to know what is normal and when it is time to seek a doctor consultation. If heavy periods are interfering with your daily life, you do not have to suffer in silence.

At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, our compassionate team is here to help. This guide explains what heavy periods are, what causes them, and the effective treatment options available to help you take back control of your life.

What are Heavy Periods (Menorrhagia)?

Menorrhagia is the medical term for menstrual bleeding that is unusually heavy or lasts for a long time.

Normally, a period lasts about 4 to 5 days, and the total blood lost is small (about 2 to 3 tablespoons). If you have menorrhagia, your period may last for more than 7 days, and you may lose twice as much blood. Over time, losing this much blood every month can cause extreme fatigue and iron-deficiency anemia, leaving you feeling weak and short of breath.

Signs It's Time for a Health Checkup

It can be hard to measure exact blood loss, but there are clear signs that your period is too heavy. You should consider scheduling a doctor consultation if you experience any of the following:

  • Soaking through one or more sanitary pads or tampons every hour, for several hours.
  • Needing to use double protection, like a pad and a tampon together.
  • Waking up during the night to change your pad or tampon.
  • Bleeding that lasts for more than a week.
  • Passing blood clots the size of a coin or larger.
  • Skipping work, social events, or normal daily activities because of your flow.
  • Feeling constantly tired, weak, or short of breath during your period.

Common Causes of Heavy Bleeding

While the exact cause is sometimes unknown, several common conditions can trigger heavy periods:

  • Hormonal Imbalances: If your hormones are off-balance, the lining of your uterus can become too thick, leading to heavy bleeding. This is common in adolescents, women nearing menopause, or those with conditions like PCOS or thyroid issues.
  • Uterine Growths: Non-cancerous growths like fibroids or polyps can make periods heavier and longer.
  • Adenomyosis: A painful condition where the uterine lining grows into the muscular walls of the uterus.
  • Intrauterine Devices (IUDs): Non-hormonal (copper) IUDs can cause heavier periods as a side effect.
  • Medications & Disorders: Certain blood thinners, anti-inflammatory drugs, or inherited bleeding disorders can increase menstrual flow.
  • Pregnancy Complications: A single, very heavy and late period could indicate a miscarriage or ectopic pregnancy.

Diagnosis at Maria Hospital

When you visit Maria Hospital for a health checkup, our goal is to understand your unique situation and provide an accurate diagnosis.

Your doctor will ask about your medical history and how your periods affect your daily routine.

Tip: It is very helpful to keep a "period diary" before your visit to track your bleeding days and pad usage!

To find the root cause, your doctor may recommend simple tests, such as:

  • Blood tests: To check for anemia, thyroid issues, or clotting problems.
  • Ultrasound: A painless scan to look for fibroids or polyps in your pelvis.
  • Pap test: To screen for infection or abnormal cells.
  • Endometrial biopsy or Hysteroscopy: Quick procedures to closely examine the lining of your uterus.

Your Treatment Options

Treatment for heavy periods depends on the underlying cause, your overall health, and whether you plan to have children in the future. Our specialists will work closely with you to find the best solution.

Medical Treatments

Medications are usually the first step to lighten your flow and relieve pain:

  • Iron Supplements: To rebuild your strength if you have anemia.
  • Pain Relievers (NSAIDs): Medicines like Ibuprofen can reduce cramps and actually decrease blood loss.
  • Tranexamic Acid: A pill taken only during your period to help your blood clot properly.
  • Hormonal Birth Control & IUDs: Pills, patches, or hormonal IUDs (like Mirena) can regulate your cycle, thin the uterine lining, and significantly lighten your periods.

Surgical Treatments

If medications do not provide enough relief, Maria Hospital offers advanced, minimally invasive surgical options:

  • Fibroid/Polyp Removal: Procedures like a myomectomy to remove specific growths causing the bleeding.
  • Uterine Artery Embolization: Cuts off the blood supply to fibroids so they shrink.
  • Endometrial Ablation: Destroys the uterine lining to greatly reduce bleeding (only for women who do not plan to become pregnant).
  • Hysterectomy: The surgical removal of the uterus. This is a permanent cure, typically considered when other treatments have failed and you no longer wish to have children.
