Missed / Late / Delayed / Paused / Absent Periods (Amenorrhea)

For many women, the menstrual cycle is a normal part of daily life. A typical cycle lasts between 21 and 35 days. However, when your period is suddenly late, skips a month, or stops completely, it is completely natural to feel worried.

The medical term for a missing period is amenorrhea. If you are experiencing this, you don't have to navigate it alone. The trusted medical team at Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, is here to help you understand your body, find the root cause, and get you back on track.

What Does a Missed Period Mean?

Missing a period is not a disease itself; it is simply a sign that something in your body has changed. Doctors usually divide this into two types:

  • Primary Amenorrhea: When a young woman reaches 15 years of age and has not yet had her first period.
  • Secondary Amenorrhea: When a woman who normally has regular periods misses them for three months (or six months for those with irregular cycles). This is the most common reason women seek a doctor consultation for missed periods.

When is it Natural to Miss a Period?

There are times in a woman's life when not having a period is completely normal. You do not need to worry if you are:

  • Not yet at the age of puberty.
  • Pregnant (this is the most common reason for a delayed cycle).
  • Breastfeeding naturally pausing your cycle.
  • Going through or have completed menopause.

Common Causes of Delayed or Absent Periods

If natural reasons don't apply to you, a missed period could be linked to lifestyle habits, hormonal changes, or other medical factors.

  • Stress and Anxiety: High mental stress affects the part of your brain that controls hormones. Once you find ways to relax, your periods will often return.
  • Weight Changes and Exercise: Your body needs enough energy to maintain a cycle. Being underweight, losing weight too fast, or doing extreme physical training can pause your periods. Similarly, carrying extra weight can also disrupt your hormone balance.
  • Hormonal Imbalances: Conditions like Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) or thyroid issues can interfere with regular ovulation.
  • Medications: Certain birth control pills, injections, or other prescription medicines can temporarily stop your periods.

Other Symptoms to Watch Out For

Depending on the cause of your missed cycle, you might notice other changes in your body. Pay attention if you also experience:

  • Unexplained weight gain or loss
  • Acne or changes in your skin
  • Hair loss, or extra hair growth on your face and body
  • Headaches or vision changes
  • Pelvic pain
  • Hot flashes

When to Book a Health Checkup in Nepal

A single late period is usually nothing to panic about. However, you should schedule a health checkup with a doctor at Maria Hospital if:

  • You are 15 and have not had your first period.
  • You miss three periods in a row.
  • You have fewer than nine periods in a whole year.
  • You notice sudden, unexplained changes to your menstrual cycle.
  • You feel severe pelvic pain along with your missed period.

Diagnosis and Treatment: What to Expect

When you visit Maria Hospital, our primary goal is to provide an accurate diagnosis in a comfortable, reassuring environment. Your doctor will gently ask about your medical history, diet, and lifestyle.

We usually start with a simple pregnancy test. Depending on your needs, we may also do blood tests to check your hormone levels or perform a painless ultrasound.

Once we understand the cause, we will create a personalized treatment plan just for you. This might include:

  • Lifestyle Adjustments: Helpful advice on balancing your diet, managing stress, or modifying your exercise routine.
  • Medications: Prescribing hormone therapy or medicines to regulate your cycle, balance your thyroid, and protect your long-term bone health.

Ignoring a persistently missed period can sometimes lead to issues with bone strength or future pregnancy. Taking care of your menstrual health is one of the best things you can do for your overall well-being.
