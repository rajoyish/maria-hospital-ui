What Is a Perineoplasty?

A perineoplasty is a surgical procedure that repairs the perineum, which is the area of skin and muscle between the vaginal opening and the anus. The main goal of this treatment is to strengthen and tighten this area while repairing any loose, scarred, or damaged skin.

Often, the perineum can get stretched or torn during childbirth, which weakens its overall foundation. This surgery reconstructs that supportive base to bring back your natural comfort.

Is It the Same as a Vaginoplasty?

Not quite. A vaginoplasty tightens the inside of the vaginal canal. A perineoplasty focuses entirely on restoring the muscles and skin at the opening of the vagina. Depending on your specific diagnosis, however, a doctor might perform both procedures at the same time.

Why Consider a Perineoplasty?

Vaginal tears from childbirth, the natural aging process, and genetics can all change how your perineal area feels and functions. A thorough doctor consultation can help figure out if this surgery is the right step for you.

Medical benefits of the treatment can include:

  • Healing painful complications caused by past vaginal tears or childbirth scars.
  • Helping to treat or prevent pelvic organ prolapse.
  • Providing lasting relief from chronic perineal pain.

Cosmetic and comfort benefits can include:

  • Removing excess, loose, or bothersome skin.
  • Improving physical sensation and friction during intimacy.
  • Reducing vaginal gas.
  • Resolving the feeling of a "wide" or loose vaginal opening.

How to Prepare and What to Expect

Before scheduling any surgery, a comprehensive health checkup is essential. During your visit, your doctor will discuss your symptoms to make sure this is the safest and best path forward. For example, if you are approaching or have already gone through menopause, your doctor might prescribe an estrogen cream for a few weeks before the surgery to strengthen the tissue and speed up healing.

During the procedure:

  • You will receive safe anesthesia so you will not feel any pain.
  • The surgeon carefully removes any excess skin and old, painful scar tissue.
  • The deeper muscles of the pelvic floor are gently repaired and stitched back together to restore their natural strength.

Your Recovery Journey

Healing takes time, and you can generally expect a full recovery in about six to eight weeks. Our team right here in Kathmandu, Nepal, will guide you through every single step of your aftercare.

What to do for a smooth recovery:

  • Rest: Plan to take it completely easy for the first week.
  • Soothe the area: Use ice packs, prescribed pain relievers, and gentle sitz baths to keep swelling and discomfort down.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink lots of water, eat high-fiber foods, and consider taking a mild stool softener. Preventing constipation is important because straining can affect your stitches.

Activity restrictions to protect your healing body:

  • No heavy lifting: Skip the strenuous exercise and heavy lifting for at least four weeks.
  • Nothing internal: Avoid using tampons or menstrual cups for six weeks.
  • Pause intimacy: Hold off on sexual intercourse for a full six weeks.

Once you are fully healed, your doctor will likely recommend simple pelvic floor exercises to keep the area strong, healthy, and supported.
