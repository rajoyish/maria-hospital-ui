What is a Clitoral Hood Reduction?

Think of the clitoral hood as a small, natural fold of skin designed to cover and protect the clitoris. For some women, this skin is naturally a bit longer, thicker, or larger than average. A clitoral hood reduction, sometimes called a hoodectomy, is simply a minor surgical procedure to gently remove that extra tissue.

Important: We want to emphasize that this procedure does not alter or remove the clitoris itself. The surgery strictly focuses on trimming the surrounding skin. The goal is just to expose the clitoris properly while keeping its natural function and delicate nerve supply completely untouched and safe.

Why Consider This Treatment?

After getting a proper diagnosis, women often choose this treatment for a mix of practical and cosmetic reasons. Here is how it can help:

  • Finding Relief from Discomfort: Extra skin can easily bunch up and rub against tight clothing, underwear, or swimsuits. By removing this tissue, we can stop the painful pinching, chafing, and irritation that might be bothering you during your daily routine or workouts.
  • Enhancing Intimacy: Sometimes, an overly large hood gets in the way and makes sexual stimulation difficult. Safely trimming back that extra skin exposes the clitoris, which can really help heighten sensitivity and improve your sexual wellness.
  • Boosting Aesthetic Confidence: It is very common for women to feel self-conscious about a noticeable bulge or an uneven appearance in their intimate area. This procedure helps create a sleeker, much more balanced look.
  • Improving Hygiene: Less extra skin means fewer folds, making it a lot easier to keep your intimate area clean and dry. In turn, this lowers your risk of dealing with irritation and annoying infections.

A quick note on labiaplasty: We often pair this procedure with a labiaplasty, which is a surgery to reduce the inner vaginal lips. Doing both at the same time is a great way to make sure the genital area looks natural and proportionate, rather than feeling top-heavy.

How is the Procedure Performed at Maria Hospital?

You will be glad to know this is a quick outpatient procedure. That means you get to go home and rest in your own bed on the very same day.

  • Time: The surgery itself is quite fast, usually wrapping up in just 30 to 60 minutes.
  • Anesthesia: We will tailor this to your comfort and specific treatment plan. We can use local anesthesia, where you stay awake but the area is completely numb, or general anesthesia if you prefer to be asleep.
  • The Technique: Our skilled surgeons use highly precise incisions to carefully trim away the extra skin. They are incredibly meticulous about avoiding any nerve endings.
  • Closure: We close the incisions using fine stitches that dissolve on their own. They will naturally disappear in about six weeks, so there is no need to worry about getting them removed.

What to Expect During Recovery

The genital area has a great blood supply, which actually helps it heal up pretty quickly. Here is a realistic look at your recovery:

  • Downtime: Plan to take it easy for 3 to 5 days at home. Most of our patients are back to their desk jobs and light, everyday activities within a week.
  • Managing Symptoms: It is completely normal to notice some mild swelling, bruising, and discomfort at first. Your doctor will prescribe pain medication and suggest using an ice pack right over your underwear to help soothe the area.
  • Hygiene: Instead of wiping after using the restroom, you will use a small squeeze bottle filled with warm water to gently rinse the area, then just pat it dry.
  • Activity Restrictions: To give your body the time it needs to heal perfectly, you will need to hold off on heavy lifting, intense workouts, swimming, baths, tampons, and sexual intercourse for about 4 to 6 weeks.

Is It Safe?

Absolutely. When performed by our experienced and qualified specialists at Maria Hospital, a clitoral hood reduction is incredibly safe. Like any surgery, there are some rare risks to be aware of, like minor bleeding, infection, or temporary swelling. But because our surgeons strictly target the skin and steer clear of the clitoral nerves, permanent loss of sensation is exceedingly rare.
