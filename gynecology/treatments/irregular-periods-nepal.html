Irregular Periods (Menstrual Irregularities) Treatment

Your menstrual cycle offers a clear window into your overall well-being. If you are experiencing unexpected changes in your period, it is completely normal to feel a bit worried or confused.

This article will help you understand what a typical cycle looks like, the common causes behind irregular periods, and how a proper diagnosis and treatment plan can help you get back on track right here in Kathmandu, Nepal.

What is a Normal Menstrual Cycle?

A menstrual cycle starts on the first day of your period and ends on the first day of your next period. For most women, a standard cycle:

  • Lasts between 21 and 35 days (28 days on average).
  • Involves 2 to 7 days of bleeding.

It is important to remember that minor changes from month to month are normal. However, if your cycle is constantly shifting or you experience severe discomfort, it might be time for a routine health checkup.

Types of Menstrual Irregularities

Periods can be irregular in a few different ways. The most common types include:

  • Missed Periods (Amenorrhea): Missing your period for three months in a row, or not starting by age 15.
  • Infrequent Periods (Oligomenorrhea): Cycles longer than 35 days, or having fewer than nine periods a year.
  • Frequent Periods (Polymenorrhea): Cycles shorter than 21 days.
  • Heavy Periods (Menorrhagia): Bleeding that lasts more than seven days or soaking through a pad every hour.
  • Light Periods (Hypomenorrhea): Very light bleeding lasting only a day or two.
  • Spotting (Metrorrhagia): Bleeding or spotting between regular periods.
  • Painful Periods (Dysmenorrhea): Severe cramps or pelvic pain that makes daily tasks difficult.

Common Causes Behind Irregular Periods

Many things can affect your cycle. Some causes are harmless, while others need a bit of medical care:

  • Life Stages: Irregularities are highly common during puberty and perimenopause (the years leading up to menopause).
  • Lifestyle: High stress, intense exercise, or sudden weight changes can disrupt your hormones.
  • PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome): A common hormonal imbalance that causes skipped or irregular cycles.
  • Thyroid Issues: An overactive or underactive thyroid gland can heavily impact your period.
  • Uterus Growths: Noncancerous growths like fibroids or polyps can cause heavy, painful bleeding.
  • Endometriosis: Tissue growing outside the uterus, causing severe pain and spotting.
  • Medications: Birth control, blood thinners, and certain steroids can change your bleeding patterns.

Why You Shouldn't Ignore Irregular Periods

While an occasionally late period is nothing to worry about, ignoring long-term changes can lead to complications:

  • Anemia: Heavy bleeding can lower your red blood cells, making you feel weak, tired, and dizzy.
  • Infertility: Irregular periods often mean irregular ovulation, making it harder to get pregnant.
  • Bone Health Issues: Missing periods frequently can lower estrogen levels, increasing the risk of weak bones (osteoporosis) later in life.
  • Future Health Risks: Hormonal conditions like PCOS are linked to a higher risk of heart disease and type 2 diabetes.

When to Seek a Doctor Consultation

You know your body best. We recommend scheduling a doctor consultation if you notice any of the following:

  • No period for three or more months (when not pregnant).
  • Cycles consistently shorter than 21 days or longer than 35 days.
  • Bleeding heavy enough to require changing a pad every hour.
  • Bleeding lasting longer than seven days.
  • Severe pain that stops you from working, studying, or doing daily activities.
  • Bleeding between periods or after sex.

Diagnosis and Treatment at Maria Hospital

Finding the exact cause of your symptoms is our top priority. During your visit to Maria Hospital, our compassionate medical team will discuss your health history and menstrual patterns.

To ensure an accurate diagnosis, we may recommend:

  • Blood Tests: To check your hormone and thyroid levels.
  • Ultrasound: A quick, painless pelvic scan to check for fibroids or cysts.

Your treatment will be tailored specifically to your needs. Options may include:

  • Lifestyle Changes: Guidance on diet, stress management, and exercise to restore balance naturally.
  • Medicines: Hormonal treatments (like birth control pills) to regulate your cycle, iron supplements for anemia, or safe pain relievers.
  • Surgical Care: Minimally invasive procedures to safely resolve issues like fibroids or polyps if needed.
