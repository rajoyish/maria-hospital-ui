Pelvic Pain Treatment

Pelvic pain can be confusing, frustrating, and disruptive to your daily life. Because the lower body holds many different organs, finding the exact cause can be tricky. At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, our experienced medical team is here to support you.

This article explains the common causes and symptoms of pelvic pain, and how a proper diagnosis and treatment plan can help you get back to feeling your best.

What is Pelvic Pain?

Pelvic pain is discomfort felt in the lower belly area, specifically below your belly button and between your hips. While often associated with women's health, it can affect anyone. Doctors generally group it into two types:

  • Acute Pelvic Pain: Sudden, intense pain that comes on quickly and goes away soon after.
  • Chronic Pelvic Pain: Pain that lasts for six months or longer and usually requires medical help to improve.

What Does It Feel Like?

The pain feels different for everyone depending on the root cause. You might experience:

  • A dull, heavy ache or feeling of pressure.
  • Sharp, stabbing, or burning sensations.
  • Cramping or throbbing that comes and goes.
  • Pain spreading to your lower back, groin, or thighs.
  • Pain triggered by exercising, using the bathroom, or intimacy.

Common Causes of Pelvic Pain

Your pelvis houses your reproductive organs, bladder, bowels, and a complex network of muscles. Pain can start in any of these areas:

  • Reproductive Issues: For women, this includes severe period cramps, endometriosis, ovarian cysts, or infections. For men, it can involve prostate inflammation.
  • Urinary Problems: Kidney stones or urinary tract infections (UTIs) are frequent causes.
  • Digestive Troubles: Conditions like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), severe constipation, or appendicitis.
  • Muscle and Nerve Stress: Tension or spasms in the pelvic floor muscles, or hernias.
  • Stress and Anxiety: Emotional stress can make physical pain feel worse or increase your sensitivity to it.

When to Seek Emergency Care

While many causes of pelvic pain are highly treatable, some symptoms require urgent medical attention. Go to the nearest emergency room if your pain is accompanied by:

  • Severe, sudden pain that rapidly worsens.
  • High fever, chills, or vomiting.
  • Dizziness, fainting, or difficulty breathing.
  • Heavy bleeding or unusual discharge.
  • Inability to go to the bathroom, or seeing blood in your urine or stool.
  • Pregnancy, or if you suspect you might be pregnant.

Getting a Diagnosis at Maria Hospital

Getting an accurate diagnosis is the first step toward feeling better. During a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital, we will carefully review your symptoms and health history.

To pinpoint the issue, your doctor may recommend a comprehensive health checkup, which could include:

  • Lab Tests: Simple blood and urine tests to check for infections or other issues.
  • Imaging Scans: Ultrasounds or CT scans to get a clear picture of your internal organs.
  • Diagnostic Procedures: Safe, minimally invasive methods to gently look inside the pelvic area if needed.

Your Treatment Options

We believe in a personalized approach to your health. Depending on what is causing your pain, your treatment plan may include:

  • Medications: Pain relievers, muscle relaxants, or antibiotics to clear up infections.
  • Hormonal Therapy: Helpful for managing women's health conditions like endometriosis or severe cramps.
  • Physical Therapy: Specialized exercises to relax and strengthen your pelvic muscles.
  • Surgery: Safe, minimally invasive surgical options for physical issues like hernias or cysts.
  • Counseling Support: Tools to help manage the mental and emotional stress of living with persistent pain.

You do not have to live with pelvic pain. If discomfort is disrupting your life, a simple doctor consultation can make all the difference.
