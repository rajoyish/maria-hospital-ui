What is a Vaginal Yeast Infection?

A vaginal yeast infection happens when there is an overgrowth of a naturally occurring fungus called Candida.

Usually, your vagina maintains a healthy balance of yeast and good bacteria. The good bacteria work hard to keep the yeast under control. However, when this delicate balance is disrupted, the yeast can multiply too quickly, leading to an uncomfortable infection. It is completely normal and is not considered a sexually transmitted infection (STI).

Common Causes: Why Do They Happen?

Any change to the natural balance of your vaginal environment can trigger an infection. Common culprits include:

  • Antibiotic Use: Antibiotics fight off bad bacteria, but they can also unintentionally clear out the "good" bacteria that keep yeast in check.
  • Hormonal Changes: Fluctuations during pregnancy, the menstrual cycle, or from birth control pills can create a favorable environment for yeast.
  • Unmanaged Diabetes: High blood sugar can increase sugar levels in vaginal fluids, feeding the yeast.
  • Scented Products: Vaginal sprays, douches, and scented soaps disrupt your natural pH balance.
  • Trapped Moisture: Yeast thrives in warm, damp places. Sitting in a wet swimsuit, sweaty gym clothes, or wearing tight synthetic underwear can promote growth.

Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

Symptoms can range from mildly annoying to severe. You might be experiencing a yeast infection if you notice:

  • Intense itching, burning, or irritation in and around the vagina.
  • A thick, white, clumpy discharge (often resembling cottage cheese) that usually has no odor.
  • A burning sensation when urinating or pain during intimacy.
  • Redness, soreness, and swelling around the vaginal opening.

Why Professional Diagnosis Matters

If you are experiencing these symptoms for the first time, or if they feel severe, please avoid trying to treat it yourself. Many symptoms of a yeast infection are identical to other conditions that require completely different medications.

Booking a doctor consultation at Maria Hospital ensures you get an accurate diagnosis. Our doctors will gently review your symptoms, perform a basic exam, and potentially test a small sample to find the exact cause of your discomfort.

Fast and Effective Treatment Options

Once diagnosed, yeast infections are easy to clear up. Depending on your specific needs, your treatment plan may include:

  • Topical Creams or Suppositories: Antifungal medications applied directly to the area for a few days to provide fast, targeted relief.
  • Oral Medication: A convenient, single-dose prescription pill to quickly clear the infection from the inside out.
  • Custom Care Plans: If you are pregnant or experience recurring infections (more than four a year), our specialists will create a long-term plan to keep you comfortable and healthy.

Easy Prevention Tips for Everyday Health

You can protect your vaginal health with a few simple, daily habits:

  • Wear Cotton Underwear: Choose breathable fabrics and avoid tight-fitting synthetic pants.
  • Stay Dry: Change out of damp clothes, swimwear, or workout gear as soon as possible.
  • Keep it Natural: Avoid douching and scented soaps. The vagina is self-cleaning, and water is usually all you need.
  • Eat Probiotics: Plain yogurt with live cultures or a daily probiotic supplement can help maintain your body's healthy bacteria.
