Menopausal Issues Treatment

Menopause is just another natural chapter in life, marking the end of your reproductive years. Officially, it begins once you haven't had a period for 12 consecutive months. But the years leading up to that milestone, known as perimenopause, can bring some unexpected and frustrating changes.

Here at Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, our Gynecology Care team is ready to help you navigate this transition comfortably. We want to help you make sense of two very common perimenopause experiences: irregular periods and hot flashes.

What to Expect with Your Menstrual Cycle

Perimenopause typically starts in your 40s. During this time, your estrogen and progesterone levels start to fluctuate unpredictably. Often, the very first clue that things are shifting is a change in your period.

Your cycles might get shorter, longer, lighter, or heavier. You might even skip a month or two. While this irregularity is completely normal, it is important to watch for certain signs that warrant a proper checkup just to be safe.

We recommend scheduling a visit with us if:

  • Your bleeding is unusually heavy or lasts longer than seven days.
  • Your periods are coming closer together than every 21 days.
  • You notice spotting between periods or bleeding after sex.
  • You experience any vaginal bleeding after you have fully reached menopause (meaning 12 full months without a period). This is not normal and needs a medical evaluation right away.

Feeling the Heat: Understanding Hot Flashes

Hot flashes are the most common symptom of menopause, affecting up to 85% of women. If you have ever felt a sudden, intense rush of heat spreading across your face, neck, and chest, you know exactly what they are.

During a hot flash, your skin might flush, and you could experience heavy sweating, a racing heart, or even a sudden wave of anxiety. When these hit at night, they are known as night sweats, and they can completely wreck your sleep, leaving you exhausted the next morning. These episodes usually last between one and five minutes. They happen because those fluctuating hormones are tricking your brain into thinking your body is overheating.

Everyday Ways to Find Relief

You don't just have to grit your teeth and endure the discomfort. A few straightforward lifestyle tweaks can actually make a big difference:

  • Keep things cool: Dress in light layers so you can easily peel something off when a hot flash hits. Keep your bedroom cool with a fan, and sip ice water when you feel the heat coming on.
  • Watch for triggers: Pay attention to what sets off a hot flash for you. Common culprits include spicy foods, hot tea or coffee, alcohol, stuffy rooms, and stress.
  • Nourish your body: Try working soy-based foods like tofu or soy milk into your meals. They contain natural plant compounds that can help balance your hormones. Fresh fruits, veggies, and healthy fats from fish are also fantastic for managing symptoms.
  • Focus on healthy habits: Regular exercise, deep breathing routines to manage stress, and maintaining a healthy weight can make you feel so much better overall.

Expert Care at Maria Hospital

If lifestyle changes just aren't cutting it and hot flashes are getting in the way of your daily life, it might be time to chat with a doctor. Our specialists offer highly effective treatment options customized for you:

  • Hormone Therapy (HT): This is one of the most effective ways to treat severe hot flashes. It works by safely replenishing your declining hormone levels. Your doctor will take a close look at your health history to make sure it is the right choice for you.
  • Non-Hormonal Options: If hormone therapy isn't a good fit, we can prescribe safe medications that don't use hormones to help cool down those flashes and improve your quality of life.
