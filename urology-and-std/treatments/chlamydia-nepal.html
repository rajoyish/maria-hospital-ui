Chlamydia Treatment

Chlamydia is one of the most common sexually transmitted infections (STIs). It is caused by bacteria and is fully curable with the right medication. However, it is often called a "silent" infection because many people do not show any obvious signs.

If you live in Kathmandu in Nepal and are concerned about your sexual health, it is important to understand the risks. Because symptoms are rare, you might have it without knowing, potentially passing it to others or facing long-term health issues.

Here is everything you need to know about symptoms, risks, and how Maria Hospital can help you with confidential testing and treatment.

How Does Chlamydia Spread?

Chlamydia is transmitted through sexual contact. You can catch it by having vaginal, anal, or oral sex with someone who has the infection.

  • Fluids: It spreads through vaginal fluid and semen.
  • No Ejaculation Needed: A man does not need to ejaculate (cum) to pass the infection to his partner.
  • Mother to Baby: A pregnant woman can pass the infection to her baby during childbirth.
  • Myth Buster: You cannot get chlamydia from casual contact like hugging, holding hands, sharing food, or sitting on a toilet seat.

Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

Most people (50% to 90%) have no symptoms at all. If symptoms do appear, they may take several weeks to show up after sex with an infected partner. If you notice any of these, you should schedule a doctor consultation immediately.

Symptoms in Women

  • Abnormal vaginal discharge (often with a strong smell).
  • A burning sensation when urinating (peeing).
  • Bleeding between periods or after sex.
  • Pain during sex or lower abdominal pain.

Symptoms in Men

  • Watery, white, or milky discharge from the penis.
  • A burning sensation when urinating.
  • Pain or swelling in the testicles (less common).

Note: Infections in the rectum or throat usually have no symptoms but can sometimes cause pain, discharge, or a sore throat.

Why Is Treatment So Important?

Even if you feel fine, leaving Chlamydia untreated can cause permanent damage to your body. Early diagnosis is key to preventing these complications:

  • Infertility in Women: Untreated chlamydia can spread to the uterus and fallopian tubes. This causes Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID), which can lead to scarring and make it difficult or impossible to get pregnant later.
  • Pregnancy Risks: It increases the risk of ectopic pregnancy (a life-threatening pregnancy outside the womb).
  • Joint Pain: In some people, it triggers reactive arthritis, causing joint pain and swelling.
  • HIV Risk: It can increase your chances of getting or transmitting HIV.

Pregnancy and Chlamydia

If you are pregnant, getting a health checkup is vital. Chlamydia can lead to premature birth. If passed to the baby during delivery, the infant may suffer from serious eye infections or pneumonia.

How Is It Diagnosed and Treated?

The good news is that testing is simple, painless, and confidential at Maria Hospital.

Simple Testing

You don't need complex procedures. A doctor usually performs a diagnosis using:

  • Urine Test: You simply pee in a cup.
  • Swab Test: A quick swab from the infected area (vagina, throat, etc.).

Effective Treatment

Chlamydia is curable. Your doctor will prescribe antibiotics (usually taken for 7 days or as a single dose).

  • Take all medicine: Finish the full course, even if symptoms stop.
  • Treat your partner: Your sexual partner(s) from the last 60 days must also get treatment to stop you from getting infected again.
  • Wait to have sex: Avoid sex until treatment is complete and symptoms are gone.

Prevention: Protect Yourself

The only 100% way to avoid Chlamydia is not to have sex. However, if you are sexually active, you can lower your risk by:

  • Using Condoms: Use latex condoms correctly every time.
  • Monogamy: Being in a relationship where both partners have been tested.
  • Regular Screening: Routine checkups help catch infections early.
