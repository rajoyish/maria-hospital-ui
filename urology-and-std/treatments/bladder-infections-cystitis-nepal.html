Bladder Infections (Cystitis) Treatment

Living in Kathmandu in Nepal, we often ignore minor aches and pains, but urinary health is something you should never overlook. A bladder infection, medically known as cystitis, is very common. It happens when your bladder becomes irritated or swollen.

While these infections can be painful and uncomfortable, the good news is that they are highly treatable. With the right doctor consultation and care at Maria Hospital, you can feel better quickly.

What Causes a Bladder Infection?

Most bladder infections happen when bacteria (germs) enter your body and travel up to the bladder.

  • Bacteria: The most common cause is E. coli bacteria, which usually lives harmlessly in the gut but causes trouble if it gets into the urinary tract.
  • Other factors: Sometimes, fungi can cause infections, especially in people with diabetes or those who have recently used antibiotics.

Who Is Most at Risk?

Anyone can get a bladder infection, but some people need to be extra careful:

  • Women: Women get them more often than men because of their anatomy. About half of all women will have one at some point.
  • Older Adults: Changes in the body as we age can increase risk.
  • People with Diabetes: High sugar levels in urine can help bacteria grow, making health checkups vital for diabetics.
  • Men with Prostate Issues: An enlarged prostate can stop the bladder from emptying fully, trapping bacteria inside.

Common Symptoms to Watch For

How do you know if you need a diagnosis? Symptoms can start suddenly. Look out for:

  • A strong urge to urinate: Even if you just went.
  • Burning sensation: Pain or burning when you pass urine.
  • Cloudy or bloody urine: The color may look different than usual.
  • Strong smell: Urine may have a foul odor.
  • Discomfort: Pressure or cramping in your lower belly or back.

Important Note for Seniors: In older adults, typical symptoms like burning might not happen. Instead, look for confusion, fever, or just feeling generally unwell.

Diagnosis and Treatment at Maria Hospital

If you have these symptoms, do not wait. A simple doctor consultation is the first step to feeling better.

How We Diagnose It

  • Urinalysis: A quick test to check your urine for signs of infection.
  • Urine Culture: If needed, we send a sample to the lab to find out exactly which bacteria is causing the problem so we can choose the best medicine.

Effective Treatment

  • Antibiotics: Doctors usually prescribe oral antibiotics to kill the bacteria. You often feel better within 24 to 48 hours, but you must finish the full course of medicine.
  • Pain Relief: We can provide medication to soothe the burning feeling while the antibiotics start working.
  • Hydration: We encourage drinking plenty of fluids to help flush the germs out.

Simple Prevention Tips

You can protect yourself with these healthy habits:

  • Drink Water: Staying hydrated is key, especially in our climate. It helps wash bacteria out of your system.
  • Hygiene: Women should always wipe from front to back to stop bacteria from spreading.
  • Post-Activity Care: Emptying your bladder after sexual activity helps flush away germs.

When Is It an Emergency?

Most bladder infections are easily cured. However, if untreated, the infection can spread to the kidneys, which is dangerous. Seek immediate help if you have:

  • High fever.
  • Pain in the side of your back (flank pain).
  • Nausea or vomiting.
  • Visible blood in the urine.
