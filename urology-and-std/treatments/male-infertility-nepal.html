Male Infertility Treatment

Trying to start a family is a beautiful journey, but it can be stressful when things don't go as planned. If you have been trying to have a baby for more than a year without success, you are not alone.

Many couples in Kathmandu in Nepal face this challenge. While people often think infertility is only a female issue, the reality is different. In about 50% of cases, the male partner plays a role.

At Maria Hospital, we want you to know that this is a shared condition, and there is hope.

What are the Signs?

The most obvious sign of male infertility is simply not being able to conceive a child. Often, there are no other visible signs. However, you should look out for:

  • Sexual difficulties: Trouble with erections or ejaculation.
  • Physical changes: Pain, swelling, or a lump in the testicle area.
  • Hormonal signs: Changes in hair growth or breast tissue.

Why Does This Happen?

Male fertility is complex. To make a baby, you need healthy sperm that can travel properly. Common causes include:

  • Sperm Issues: This is the most common cause. You might have a low sperm count, or the sperm might not move correctly.
  • Varicocele: This is a swelling of the veins that drain the testicle. It raises the temperature in the scrotum and can damage sperm quality.
  • Infections: Past infections or sexually transmitted issues can block the passage of sperm.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Smoking, heavy alcohol use, and taking steroids for bodybuilding can severely harm your fertility.
  • Environmental Factors: Frequent exposure to high heat (like hot tubs) or certain chemicals.

How We Diagnose the Problem

Diagnosis is the first step toward a solution. We recommend that both partners come for a doctor consultation at the same time. When you visit us for a health checkup, here is what to expect:

  1. Medical History & Exam: Your doctor will ask about your lifestyle and history and perform a simple physical check.
  2. Semen Analysis: This is the most important test. We analyze a sample in our lab to check the count, shape, and movement of your sperm.
  3. Blood Tests: We check your hormone levels to see if your body is producing sperm correctly.
  4. Ultrasound: If needed, we use imaging to look for blockages or swollen veins.

Effective Treatments Available in Nepal

The good news is that many causes of male infertility can be fixed. At Maria Hospital, we offer a range of options for diagnosis and treatment:

1. Lifestyle Changes

Simple changes can make a big difference in your sperm health:

  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol.
  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Avoid hot environments (like saunas) to keep testicles cool.

2. Medical and Surgical Help

  • Medicine: Antibiotics can treat infections, and hormone therapy can boost sperm production.
  • Surgery: A simple procedure can fix swollen veins (varicocele) or repair blockages.

We understand that infertility can cause stress, depression, and strain on a relationship. These feelings are normal. We encourage you to talk openly with your partner and seek support.

Early diagnosis can lead to successful treatment. Don't let fear stop you from starting your family.
