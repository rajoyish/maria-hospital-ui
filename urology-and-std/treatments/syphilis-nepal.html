Syphilis Treatment

Syphilis is a common sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by bacteria. It is often called the "great imitator" because its symptoms can look like many other illnesses.

If you are worried about symptoms, take a deep breath. The most important thing to know is that syphilis is completely curable with the right medication. How Do You Get Syphilis?

Syphilis is spread through direct contact with a syphilis sore during sex (vaginal, anal, or oral). These sores can appear on the genitals, rectum, or lips and mouth.

It is important to know that you CANNOT get syphilis from:

  • Toilet seats or doorknobs
  • Swimming pools or hot tubs
  • Sharing clothes or eating utensils

The 4 Stages of Syphilis

Syphilis happens in stages. You might have the infection for years without knowing it, which is why a regular health checkup is so important.

1. Primary Stage (The First Sign)

  • What happens: A small sore (called a chancre) appears where the bacteria entered your body.
  • What it looks like: The sore is usually round, firm, and painless.
  • Timing: It appears about 3 weeks after exposure.
  • Healing: It heals on its own in 3 to 6 weeks, but the infection is still inside you. You need treatment to stop it from getting worse.

2. Secondary Stage (The Rash)

  • The Rash: You may get a rough, red rash on the palms of your hands or the bottoms of your feet. It usually does not itch.
  • Flu-like Symptoms: You might feel tired, have a fever, sore throat, swollen glands, or muscle aches.
  • Warning: Like the first stage, these signs will go away without medicine, but the infection remains dangerous.

3. Latent Stage (The Hidden Stage)

  • There are no visible signs during this stage.
  • Without treatment, the bacteria can sleep in your body for years.

4. Tertiary Stage (The Late Stage)

  • This creates serious health problems 10 to 30 years later.
  • It can damage the heart, brain, liver, bones, and joints. It can be life-threatening.

Serious Complications & Pregnancy

If left untreated, syphilis can spread to the brain (Neurosyphilis), eyes (Ocular Syphilis), or ears.

For Pregnant Women:

Syphilis is dangerous for unborn babies. It can cause miscarriage or serious health issues for the baby after birth.

Our Advice: Every pregnant woman should book a diagnosis and screening at their first prenatal visit.

Testing and Diagnosis

The only way to know for sure if you have syphilis is to get tested. At Maria Hospital, we use simple and quick methods:

  • Blood Tests: To check for antibodies.
  • Fluid Testing: Checking fluid from a sore.

You should book a doctor consultation if you have symptoms, if your partner has tested positive, or if you simply want peace of mind.

Treatment: Simple and Effective

Syphilis is curable.

  • Antibiotics: The standard treatment is Penicillin.
  • Early Stages: Usually, a single injection is enough to cure the infection.
  • Later Stages: You may need additional doses.

Note: Treatment cures the infection and stops future damage, but it cannot repair permanent damage already done to your organs. This is why early testing is vital.

Prevention

  • Use Condoms: Correctly using latex condoms lowers your risk.
  • Be Open: If you are diagnosed, let your partner know so they can also get treated.
  • Regular Screening: Regular checkups are the best way to stay safe.
