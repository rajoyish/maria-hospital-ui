Non-gonococcal Urethritis (NGU) Treatment

Are you experiencing discomfort when you urinate or noticing unusual discharge? You might be dealing with Non-gonococcal Urethritis (NGU). While the name sounds complicated, it is simply an infection and inflammation of the urethra (the tube that carries urine out of the body).

At Maria Hospital in Kathmandu, we understand that sexual health issues can be worrying. This article will help you understand NGU, identify the signs, and know when to book a doctor consultation.

What is NGU?

NGU stands for Non-gonococcal Urethritis.

  • Urethritis means the urine tube is inflamed or swollen.
  • Non-gonococcal means the infection is caused by germs other than Gonorrhea.

It is the most common sexually transmitted infection (STI) in men, but it can affect women too.

What Causes NGU?

Most of the time, NGU is passed through sexual contact. However, there are other causes too.

  • Bacteria: The most common cause is a bacteria called Chlamydia. Another common cause is Mycoplasma.
  • Viruses: Herpes (HSV) and other viruses can sometimes cause it.
  • Non-Sexual Causes: Urinary tract infections (UTIs), injury to the private parts, or irritation from medical tubes (catheters).
  • Unknown Causes: Sometimes, no specific germ is found, but the inflammation still needs treatment.

Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

Symptoms usually show up 1 to 3 weeks after infection. However, many people have no symptoms at all but can still pass the infection to others.

Symptoms in Men

  • Discharge: White, cloudy, or watery liquid coming from the penis.
  • Pain: A burning or stinging feeling when peeing.
  • Irritation: Itching or soreness at the tip of the penis.
  • Staining: You might see discharge stains on your underwear.

Symptoms in Women

Women often have no symptoms. If they do appear, they may include:

  • Unusual vaginal discharge.
  • Pain or burning when urinating.
  • Pain in the lower stomach (abdomen).

Note: If you notice any of these signs, do not wait. A timely health checkup at Maria Hospital can prevent complications.

  2. Test: Take a urine sample or a gentle swab to check for bacteria.
  3. Rule Out Gonorrhea: We ensure it isn’t Gonorrhea or other STIs so we can give you the right medicine.

Treatment Options

The good news is that NGU is curable. Treatment typically involves antibiotics to kill the bacteria.

  • Medication: Doctors usually prescribe antibiotics like Doxycycline (for 7 days) or Azithromycin (a single dose or short course).
  • Finish Your Medicine: Even if the pain stops, you must finish all your pills to ensure the infection is fully gone.
  • Treat Your Partner: Your sexual partner(s) must be tested and treated at the same time, or you might pass the infection back and forth.
  • No Sex During Treatment: Avoid sex for at least 7 days until treatment is finished and symptoms are gone.

What if it comes back?

If symptoms return, it might be because the bacteria were resistant to the medicine, or you were re-infected. In this case, return to Maria Hospital immediately for a follow-up. We may need to prescribe a different antibiotic.

Why You Should Not Ignore NGU

If left untreated, NGU can lead to serious health problems in Nepal:

  • In Men: It can cause painful swelling of the testicles (epididymitis), which can lead to infertility (inability to have children).
  • In Women: It can cause Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID), which can damage reproductive organs and lead to infertility or dangerous pregnancies.

Prevention Tips

  • Safe Sex: Use condoms correctly every time you have sex.
  • Monogamy: Being in a faithful relationship with one uninfected partner reduces risk.
  • Regular Testing: If you have a new partner or symptoms, get tested.
