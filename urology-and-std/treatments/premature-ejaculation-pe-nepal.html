Premature Ejaculation (PE) Treatment

Do you feel like you "finish" too quickly during intimacy? If so, please know that you are not alone.

Premature Ejaculation (PE) is a medical condition where a man ejaculates sooner than he or his partner wants. It is actually the most common sexual health issue for men, affecting nearly 40% of the male population at some point.

At Maria Hospital, located in the heart of Kathmandu in Nepal, we know that this condition can cause stress, embarrassment, and relationship trouble. But we also know the most important fact: PE is treatable, and it is not a permanent flaw.

What Exactly is Premature Ejaculation?

While it is normal to finish early occasionally, doctors generally consider it a medical condition if three things happen:

  • Timing: Ejaculation happens within one minute (or less) of starting intercourse.
  • No Control: You feel like you cannot delay or stop it from happening.
  • Distress: It causes you frustration, anxiety, or makes you avoid intimacy with your partner.

Why Does It Happen?

The causes of PE are often a mix of your physical health and your mindset. It is rarely just "one thing."

Psychological Factors

  • Anxiety: Nervousness about performance or being with a new partner.
  • Relationship Stress: Unresolved conflicts or lack of communication.
  • Depression: Low mood or guilt can affect sexual performance.

Physical Factors

  • Serotonin Levels: Chemical imbalances in the brain can speed up ejaculation.
  • Prostate Health: Infection or inflammation in the prostate.
  • Erectile Issues: Men worried about losing an erection might rush, creating a habit of finishing fast.

Effective Treatment Options

The good news is that most men see great improvement with the right help. Treatment usually combines simple techniques, medication, or counseling.

1. Behavioral Techniques

These are methods to help you "unlearn" the habit of rushing:

  • The Stop-Start Method: Stimulation is stopped just before you feel you are about to finish, allowing the feeling to pass before starting again. [Image of stop-start technique for premature ejaculation]
  • The Squeeze Method: Gently squeezing the head of the penis to reduce the urge to ejaculate. [Image of squeeze technique for premature ejaculation]
  • Pelvic Floor Exercises: Strengthening the muscles used to stop urination (Kegels) can give you better control. [Image of male pelvic floor muscles for kegel exercises]

2. Medications

Your doctor at Maria Hospital may prescribe safe medications to help:

  • Oral Medication: Certain medicines can boost serotonin levels to delay ejaculation.
  • Numbing Creams/Sprays: These reduce sensitivity slightly to help you last longer.
  • ED Treatment: If you also have Erectile Dysfunction, treating that often solves the PE issue too.

3. Counseling

Since anxiety is a big factor, speaking to a specialist can help build your confidence and reduce performance pressure.
