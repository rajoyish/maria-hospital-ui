What is Erectile Dysfunction?

Simply put, Erectile Dysfunction is the inability to get or keep an erection firm enough for sexual intercourse.

While it is normal to have occasional trouble due to stress or tiredness, ED is considered a medical condition when:

  • It happens routinely.
  • It progressively gets worse over time.

Did you know? Although ED is more common in older men (affecting over 50% of men aged 40–70), it is not just a natural part of aging. It is a treatable condition.

Common Symptoms to Watch For

If you are worried about your sexual health, look out for these common signs:

  • Difficulty getting an erection before sex.
  • Trouble keeping an erection during sexual activity.
  • Reduced sexual desire (low libido).

What Causes ED?

It is rarely "just in your head." In fact, ED is often the body’s way of warning you about other underlying health problems.

1. Physical Health Issues

An erection requires healthy blood flow. Therefore, conditions that block or damage your blood vessels are the most common causes.

  • Heart Health: High blood pressure and clogged arteries are major triggers. ED can sometimes be an early warning sign of heart disease.
  • Diabetes: This is very common in Nepal. Men with diabetes are at high risk because high blood sugar damages nerves and blood vessels.
  • Lifestyle: Smoking, heavy alcohol use, obesity, and lack of exercise can all lead to ED.
  • Medications: Some medicines for blood pressure or depression may affect your ability to get an erection.

2. Psychological Factors

Your brain plays a big role in intimacy. Emotional triggers include:

  • Stress and anxiety (especially fear of "performing" well).
  • Depression.
  • Relationship conflicts.

Effective Treatment Options

The good news is that ED is highly treatable. We use a "stepwise" approach, starting with the simplest solutions.

1. Lifestyle Changes

Better general health leads to better sexual health. We may recommend:

  • Quitting smoking.
  • Losing weight through diet.
  • Increasing exercise (which improves blood flow and vascular health).

2. Oral Medications

This is the most common treatment. Drugs like sildenafil (Viagra®) and tadalafil (Cialis®) help relax muscles to increase blood flow during stimulation.

Important Safety Warning: Never take these pills without a prescription, especially if you take nitrate medication for chest pain. Always consult a doctor first.

3. Advanced Medical Treatments

If pills do not work, we offer other effective options:

Injections
Medication delivered directly to the area to create an erection quickly.
Vacuum Devices
A safe pump system that draws blood into the penis.
Implants
A surgical solution with high satisfaction rates for men who do not respond to other treatments.
