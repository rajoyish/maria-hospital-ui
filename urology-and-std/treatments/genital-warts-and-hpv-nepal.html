Genital Warts and HPV Infection Treatment

Noticing changes in your body, especially in private areas, can be worrying. However, it is important to know that you are not alone. Human Papillomavirus (HPV) is one of the most common infections worldwide, and we see many cases here in Kathmandu, Nepal.

At Maria Hospital, we believe that understanding your health is the first step toward feeling better. This article explains the symptoms of genital warts, how they spread, and the effective treatment options available to you.

What Are Genital Warts?

Genital warts are soft growths that appear on the skin around the genitals or anus. They are caused by the HPV virus. While the idea of a virus sounds scary, it is helpful to know that the types of HPV that cause warts are usually "low-risk." This means they generally do not cause cancer.

Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

Genital warts might appear weeks, months, or even years after contact with the virus. Sometimes, the virus is "silent" and shows no symptoms at all. However, if symptoms do appear, look out for:

  • Small Bumps: These can be skin-colored, grey, or red.
  • Cauliflower Shape: Irregular clusters of warts that look like a small cauliflower.
  • Location: In women, they may appear on the vulva, cervix, or anus. In men, they often appear on the penis, scrotum, or anus.
  • Discomfort: While usually painless, they can sometimes cause itching, burning, or bleeding during intercourse.

If you notice any unusual lumps or changes, a private doctor consultation is the best way to get answers.

How Does It Spread?

HPV is highly contagious. It spreads primarily through skin-to-skin contact during vaginal, anal, or oral sex.

  • No fluids required: Unlike some other infections, HPV passes just through touching infected skin.
  • No visible signs needed: A person can pass the virus even if they don't have visible warts.
  • Myth buster: You typically cannot get genital warts from toilet seats, towels, or cutlery.

Diagnosis: How We Check for HPV

At Maria Hospital, our priority is your comfort and privacy. A diagnosis is usually quick and straightforward:

  • Visual Exam: Our doctors can often diagnose warts simply by looking at them during a physical health checkup.
  • Acetowhite Test: Sometimes, a mild vinegar solution is used to make the infected tissue turn white, making it easier to see.
  • Biopsy: In rare cases, a tiny piece of tissue may be sent to a lab to be sure.
  • Pap Smears: For women, routine Pap tests are essential to screen for cervical health, though they check for cell changes rather than visible warts.

Safe and Effective Treatment Options

While the virus itself doesn't have a cure, the warts can be removed. It is very important not to use over-the-counter wart removers from a medical shop, as these can damage sensitive skin.

Your doctor at Maria Hospital may recommend:

  • Prescription Creams: Medications like Imiquimod or Podofilox that boost your immune system or stop wart growth.
  • Cryotherapy (Freezing): Using liquid nitrogen to freeze the wart so it falls off.
  • Electrocautery: Using a gentle electric current to remove the wart.
  • Laser Therapy: Used for warts that are difficult to treat.

Can HPV Be Prevented?

Yes, you can lower your risk significantly:

  • Vaccination: The HPV vaccine (like Gardasil 9) is safe and highly recommended.
  • Condoms: Using condoms helps lower the risk, though it doesn't provide 100% protection since the virus lives on skin.
  • Lifestyle: Smoking weakens the immune system. Quitting smoking helps your body fight off the virus naturally.

A Note on Pregnancy and Cancer

  • Cancer Risk: The HPV types that cause genital warts (types 6 and 11) rarely lead to cancer. However, regular screenings are still important for overall health.
  • Pregnancy: Most women with HPV have healthy pregnancies and babies. C-sections are generally not required unless warts block the birth canal.

When Should You See a Doctor?

If you or your partner notice bumps, itching, or any discomfort in the genital area, do not wait. While it can feel embarrassing, genital warts are a very common medical condition that we treat regularly in Kathmandu, Nepal. Early diagnosis leads to faster relief and peace of mind.
