Urology and STDs Care

Maria Hospital's Urology and STD Departments provide comprehensive diagnosis and treatment for diseases related to the kidneys, bladder, urinary tract, and male reproductive system, as well as sexually transmitted diseases (STDs).

Our experienced physicians are committed to early diagnosis, safe treatment, and confidential counseling. We understand that urological and sexual health issues can be sensitive topics; therefore, our team always adheres to principles of respect and inclusivity, ensuring that patient privacy is fully protected.

Urology Care

We diagnose and treat a wide range of urinary tract infections and symptoms, including:

  • Phimosis (Tight Foreskin): A medical condition characterized by an overly tight foreskin that cannot be fully retracted, which can lead to painful urination, poor hygiene, recurring infections, and discomfort during intimacy.
  • Urinary tract infection (UTI): A medical condition characterized by a bacterial infection in any part of the urinary system, which can lead to painful urination, a frequent urge to void, cloudy or discolored urine, and pelvic pain.
  • Frequent Urination: A medical condition characterized by the persistent need to empty the bladder more often than usual, which can lead to nocturia, disrupted sleep, work interruptions, and decreased daily happiness.
  • Painful Urination (Dysuria): A medical condition characterized by inflammation or irritation of the urinary tract, which can lead to a sharp burning sensation, a frequent urge to urinate, cloudy or bloody urine, and unusual discharge.
  • Blood in Urine (Hematuria): A medical condition characterized by the leakage of blood cells into the urinary tract, which can lead to visibly discolored urine, pain or burning during urination, a frequent and sudden urge to urinate, and back or side pain.
  • Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH): A medical condition characterized by a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate gland that compresses the urethra, which can lead to frequent urination, a sudden urge to urinate, a weak urine stream, and a feeling of incomplete bladder emptying.
  • Prostatitis: A medical condition characterized by the inflammation or swelling of the prostate gland located just below the bladder, which can lead to painful urination, frequent nighttime urination, pelvic and groin discomfort, and pain during ejaculation.
  • Bladder Infections (Cystitis): A medical condition characterized by an infection or inflammation of the bladder, which can lead to a frequent and sudden urge to urinate, a burning sensation during urination, cloudy or bloody urine, and lower abdominal discomfort.
  • Difficulty Urinating (Urinary Hesitancy) or Weak Urine Stream: A medical condition characterized by a delay in starting urination caused by bladder muscles taking extra time to build pressure or physical obstructions in the urinary tract, which can lead to a weak urine stream, intermittent flow, the need to strain, and a feeling of incomplete bladder emptying.
  • Urethral Stricture (Narrowing of the urethra): A medical condition characterized by the narrowing of the urethra due to scar tissue, which can lead to a weak urine flow, a feeling of incomplete bladder emptying, a spraying urine stream, and pain while urinating.
  • Male Infertility (Low sperm count, poor sperm quality): A medical condition characterized by abnormalities in sperm health, production, or transport, which can lead to an inability to conceive a child, trouble with erections or ejaculation, pain or swelling in the testicle area, and hormonal changes.
  • Erectile Dysfunction (ED)/Impotent: A medical condition characterized by the reduction or interruption of normal blood flow into the corpus cavernosum, which can lead to difficulty achieving an erection, trouble maintaining firmness during sexual activity, reduced sexual desire, and emotional stress.
  • Premature Ejaculation (PE): A medical condition characterized by a rapid and involuntary ejaculatory response often linked to altered nerve signals, brain chemical imbalances, or prostate inflammation, which can lead to ejaculation within one minute of starting intercourse, an inability to delay climax, severe performance anxiety, and relationship stress.
  • Testicular Pain and Swelling: A medical condition characterized by inflammation, fluid accumulation, or the twisting of blood vessels within the scrotum, which can lead to sudden severe pain, a visibly red and enlarged scrotum, nausea or vomiting, and potential permanent testicular damage.

Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs) Care

Our STD department offers discreet testing, consultation, and treatmentfor the following conditions:

  • Gonorrhea: A medical condition characterized by a bacterial sexually transmitted infection affecting the reproductive organs, rectum, throat, or eyes, which can lead to painful urination, unusual genital discharge, pelvic pain, and swollen testicles.
  • Chlamydia
  • Syphilis
  • Genital Herpes
  • Genital Warts and HPV (Human Papillomavirus)
  • Trichomoniasis
  • Non-gonococcal Urethritis (NGU)
  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) (Associated with STIs)

When to see a Doctor?

If you experience vaginal discharge, itching, ulcers, burning, or pain during urination, please visit the Urology and STDs Department at Maria Hospital for appropriate evaluation and treatment.
