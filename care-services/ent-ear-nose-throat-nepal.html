ENT (Ear, Nose & Throat) Care

Maria Hospital’s ENT department is here to help you with ear, nose, and throat problems. We use modern equipment and safe treatments to help you breathe, hear, and speak clearly again.

We treat everyone—from young children to older adults—in a friendly and comfortable setting.

Ear Care

  • Ear Infections (Acute and Chronic): A medical condition characterized by fluid buildup and swelling in the middle ear behind the eardrum, which can lead to sharp pain, persistent pressure, sleep disruption, and muffled hearing.
  • Ear Pain: A medical condition characterized by discomfort originating from the structures of the ear or referred from shared nerves in the jaw and throat, which can lead to sharp or dull aches, a feeling of internal pressure, difficulty chewing, and sleep disruption.
  • Hearing Loss and Age-Related Hearing Problems: A medical condition characterized by gradual damage to the inner ear nerves, which can lead to difficulty understanding speech in noisy environments, an inability to perceive high-pitched sounds, frequent ringing in the ears, and a reliance on excessively high audio volume.
  • Ear Discharge (Otorrhea): A medical condition characterized by the abnormal leakage of fluid, pus, or blood from the ear canal, which can lead to foul-smelling drainage, ear canal swelling, eardrum ruptures, and hearing loss.
  • Ear Wax (Cerumen) Blockage: A medical condition characterized by the buildup and hardening of wax inside the ear canal, which can lead to muffled hearing, ear fullness, earaches, and tinnitus.
  • Tinnitus: A medical condition characterized by changes in the ear or auditory nerve that cause the brain to misinterpret sound signals, which can lead to persistent ringing or buzzing sounds, disrupted sleep, impaired focus, and anxiety.
  • Vertigo, Dizziness and Balance Disorders: A medical condition characterized by a mismatch of sensory signals sent to the brain from the inner ear, eyes, and muscles, which can lead to a spinning sensation, unsteadiness, nausea, and blurred vision.
  • Perforated / Ruptured Eardrum (Tympanic Membrane Perforation): A medical condition characterized by a small hole or tear in the thin tissue separating the outer ear from the middle ear, which can lead to sudden ear pain, fluid drainage, muffled hearing, and dizziness.
  • Otitis Media and Otitis Externa: Otitis media and otitis externa are medical conditions characterized by inflammation or infection of the middle ear space or the external ear canal, which can lead to severe ear pain, localized swelling, fluid discharge, and temporary hearing loss.
