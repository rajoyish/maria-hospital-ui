Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Review our hospital FAQs to understand our transparent billing, confidential consultation processes, and specialized clinical treatments prior to your visit.

What is the OPD charge at Maria Hospital?

The initial OPD (Outpatient Department) form fee is NPR 500. Following the doctor's comprehensive clinical assessment, any subsequent treatment costs are determined transparently based on your specific medical history, physical examination, and required diagnostic lab or imaging tests.

What is the cost of laser surgery for piles?

Surgical pricing varies depending on the severity and grading of the anorectal condition. After the NPR 500 OPD consultation, our proctology specialists will provide a highly accurate, itemized estimate for laser surgery with no hidden fees.

Do you treat STDs confidentially?

Yes, entirely. We strictly adhere to patient confidentiality laws. STD testing and medical consultations are conducted privately, without judgment, ensuring absolute discretion regarding your sexual health and electronic medical records.

Do I need an appointment or can I walk in?

Walk-in emergency and standard OPD patients are always welcome. However, booking an appointment in advance guarantees minimal wait times and allows our clinical staff to allocate dedicated, private consultation time for you.

Does Maria Hospital offer infertility treatments?

Our Gynecology and Urology departments collaborate to diagnose root causes of infertility, such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) or low sperm count. Treatment regimens are then customized based on advanced diagnostic and endocrine test results.

Are the doctors at Maria Hospital board-certified?

Absolutely. Every specialist at Maria Hospital holds recognized board certifications and possesses extensive clinical experience. Our medical team frequently utilizes evidence-based practices to ensure the highest standard of patient safety and care.
