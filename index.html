Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

FAQs answer common visitor questions to make your visit seamless. Review them beforehand to save time and learn basic information about us.

What is the OPD charge at Maria Hospital?

The OPD form fee is NPR 500; after the doctor's assessment, the total treatment cost is decided based on history, examination, and any required lab/imaging tests.

What's the price for treatment of X or Y?

Pricing depends on the patient's treatment history, background, physical examination, and lab tests; the OPD form fee is NPR 500, and final cost is confirmed after doctor evaluation.

Do you treat STDs confidentially?

Yes, consultations are handled professionally with patient privacy and respectful communication; tests and treatment are advised based on symptoms and risk history.

Can I come directly or need an appointment?

Both options work—walk-in OPD is available, and booking in advance helps reduce waiting time (recommended for privacy and time planning).

Does Maria Hospital cure infertility?

Gynecology mainly supports infertility-related concerns such as sperm count issues and irregular menstruation; the doctor advises investigations and a treatment plan based on the couple's reports and tests.

Are Maria Hospital doctors experienced?

Yes, Maria Hospital has professional and experienced doctors, and the hospital promotes access to specialist clinicians and modern technology.
