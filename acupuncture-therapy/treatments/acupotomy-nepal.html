What is Acupotomy?

Often called "knife-needle therapy," Acupotomy is a unique treatment that beautifully blends the ancient healing wisdom of Traditional Chinese Medicine with modern medical science.

Instead of a traditional round acupuncture needle, our specialists use a tiny tool called an "acupotome." This instrument has a flat, blade-like tip that acts as a microscopic scalpel. It reaches deep into the tissue to target the exact source of your pain, completely avoiding the need for large incisions.

How Does It Relieve Pain?

When muscles, tendons, or joints get injured over time, your body creates scar tissue or tight "knots" to try and heal the area. The problem is that this scar tissue can end up trapping nerves, restricting your movement, and blocking healthy blood flow. This often leads to severe and ongoing pain.

During your doctor consultation, our highly trained practitioners will carefully use the acupotome to pinpoint these tight spots. By gently releasing the restrictive scar tissue, Acupotomy helps your body heal in three key ways:

  • Relieves Pressure: It frees trapped nerves and blood vessels to instantly reduce tension and numbness.
  • Improves Blood Flow: It delivers fresh oxygen and vital nutrients directly to the injured area to speed up recovery.
  • Stops Inflammation: It kickstarts your body's natural healing response and effectively breaks the cycle of chronic pain.

What Conditions Can Acupotomy Treat?

Acupotomy is well known for relieving pain that might feel impossible to cure. After a thorough diagnosis at our clinic, we might recommend this therapy for a variety of issues:

  • Back and Neck Pain: Severe neck stiffness, lower back pain, and slipped discs.
  • Joint Issues: Frozen shoulder, general joint stiffness, and knee arthritis.
  • Nerve and Tendon Problems: Tennis elbow, carpal tunnel syndrome, and trigger finger (a condition where a finger gets stuck in a bent position).
  • Other Conditions: Migraines, chronic tension headaches, and bodily tension caused by nerve issues.

Why Choose Acupotomy?

Many of our patients choose Acupotomy as a safer and highly effective alternative to traditional open surgery. Some of the biggest benefits include:

  • It is Minimally Invasive: The treatment leaves only a tiny mark that looks a lot like a standard needle prick.
  • It is Fast: The procedure is incredibly quick. It often takes just seconds per treatment point.
  • It Offers High Success Rates: It frequently provides lasting relief even when standard massages, injections, or painkillers have stopped working.

Is Acupotomy Safe?

Absolutely. When performed by our qualified healthcare professionals, Acupotomy is an incredibly safe procedure.

It is completely normal to experience some mild soreness or minor bruising after your session, but these effects usually fade within a few days. We just ask that you keep the treated area clean and dry for 48 hours and hold off on bathing immediately afterward.

Please note: Acupotomy is not recommended for pregnant women or individuals with active fevers, skin infections, or bleeding disorders.
