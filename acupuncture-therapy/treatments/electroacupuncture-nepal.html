What is Electroacupuncture?

Think of electroacupuncture as a modern upgrade to traditional acupuncture. A trained professional carefully places very fine, sterile needles into specific healing points on your body.

The big difference is what happens next. We attach tiny clips to those needles and connect them to a special device. This machine sends a very mild, soothing electrical pulse between the needles. This gives our therapists the ability to offer a steady and controlled treatment, which often works even better than traditional methods alone.

What to Expect During Your Doctor Consultation

We want you to be completely comfortable. During your visit, you will just sit or lie down in a relaxing space. Once the needles are set, the practitioner will turn the device on to its lowest setting and adjust it until it feels exactly right for you.

You won’t feel any painful shocks. Instead, you will probably just notice a gentle:

  • Humming
  • Tingling
  • Vibrating
  • Buzzing feeling

Sessions are quick, usually lasting only 10 to 30 minutes. Afterward, it is perfectly normal to feel deeply relaxed or even a little sleepy. We always suggest taking it easy and drinking plenty of water for the rest of the day.

Is Electroacupuncture Safe?

Absolutely! When done by our caring and qualified professionals, electroacupuncture is incredibly safe. We use a very low electrical current and monitor you closely the whole time. Side effects are rare and very mild. You might see a tiny bruise where a needle was, feel a bit dizzy, or notice a lingering tingle that fades quickly.

Who Should Avoid This Therapy?

Because we do use mild electricity, this treatment is not meant for everyone. You should skip electroacupuncture if you:

  • Have a pacemaker or any other implanted electrical medical device.
  • Have a history of seizures or epilepsy.
  • Deal with severe heart disease or irregular heartbeats.
  • Are pregnant, unless an experienced specialist specifically recommends it for pregnancy-related pain.
