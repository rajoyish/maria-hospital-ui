Caudal Epidural Infusions

Dealing with intense lower back pain or shooting leg pain can make even the simplest daily tasks feel completely overwhelming. If you are struggling with this kind of discomfort, the team at Maria Hospital right here in Kathmandu is ready to help. We offer a highly effective and safe treatment known as a caudal epidural infusion.

Often called a caudal epidural steroid injection, this is a quick, minimally invasive procedure that delivers relief right where it hurts near your tailbone. If you are tired of living with pain, booking a consultation with one of our doctors is a great place to start.

What Conditions Can This Treat?

Getting the right diagnosis is the first step to finally feeling better. A specialist at Maria Hospital might suggest this treatment if you are dealing with:

  • Sciatica: That sharp, shooting pain, numbness, or tingling that travels from your lower back down your legs.
  • Bulging or Herniated Discs: This happens when the soft cushions between your spinal bones press outward and pinch the surrounding nerves.
  • Spinal Stenosis: A condition where the spine narrows and puts painful pressure on your nerves.
  • Post-Surgery Pain: Lingering pain that just will not go away after a past back surgery.

As a quick note, this approach is so gentle that it is also safely used to help children manage pain during certain surgeries.

How Does the Treatment Work?

The procedure uses a simple but effective combination of two medications to keep you comfortable:

  • A Local Anesthetic: This is a numbing agent that works almost immediately to give you quick, short-term relief and break the cycle of pain.
  • An Anti-inflammatory Steroid: This medicine goes to work on the swelling around your irritated nerves. It might take a few days for you to feel the full effect, but it offers long-lasting relief that can stick around for weeks or even months.

What to Expect During Your Visit

There is no need to pack an overnight bag. This is an outpatient procedure, meaning you will be able to go home the exact same day. Better yet, the whole process usually only takes about 15 to 30 minutes.

  1. Getting Ready: You will just lie back and get comfortable on your stomach.
  2. Numbing the Area: Our team will clean the area around your tailbone and give you a quick numbing injection so you do not feel any sharp pain during the process.
  3. Precision Placement: Using advanced ultrasound or X-ray imaging, your doctor will carefully guide a very thin needle right to the problem area.
  4. The Infusion: Finally, the soothing medication is slowly injected to calm those inflamed nerves down.

Recovery and Aftercare

Once the infusion is done, you will relax in our recovery room for about 30 to 60 minutes so our nursing staff can keep an eye on you. Your legs might feel a little heavy or numb right afterward, so you will definitely need a friend or family member to drive you home.

We suggest taking it easy for the first day or two. Skip the heavy lifting and avoid driving. Just keep in mind that as the initial numbing medicine wears off, you might feel a bit of your original pain return. Do not worry, because this is completely normal. The steroid will kick in a few days later to provide deeper, lasting relief.

Are There Any Side Effects?

These infusions are very safe, and serious complications are incredibly rare. You might experience a few mild and temporary side effects, such as:

  • A little soreness or bruising right where you got the injection.
  • That temporary heavy feeling in your legs we mentioned earlier.
  • Mild nausea, stomach cramps, or a flushed face from the steroid.

If you have diabetes, the steroid could temporarily raise your blood sugar levels. Our team will walk you through exactly how to monitor this safely at home.
