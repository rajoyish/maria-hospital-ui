What is Tui Na Massage?

Pronounced "twee nah," this ancient healing practice has been around for over 2,000 years. The name literally means "push and grasp," perfectly describing the dynamic hand movements our specialists use.

This is not your average relaxing spa massage. Tui Na is a targeted medical treatment. Our practitioners use pressing, kneading, rolling, and stretching motions to work on soft tissues, muscles, and tendons.

Traditional medicine suggests that pain occurs when your body's energy, known as Qi, gets blocked. Tui Na helps clear those blockages and improves blood circulation. This allows your body to heal itself from the inside out.

What is TCM Bone Setting?

Bone setting, or Zheng Gu, is a specialized physical therapy designed to realign your bones and joints. While Tui Na focuses on the muscles, bone setting corrects structural issues, treats dislocated joints, and takes the pressure off pinched nerves.

If the idea of bone setting makes you nervous, there is no need to worry. Unlike some of the more aggressive chiropractic adjustments you might have seen, TCM bone setting at Maria Hospital is gentle and holistic. Our experts use controlled and gradual movements that always respect your body's natural limits.

How Do Tui Na and Bone Setting Work Together?

We rarely use bone setting entirely on its own. Instead, our specialists blend both therapies to give you the best possible results.

First, we start with a warm up. The practitioner uses Tui Na massage to loosen tight muscles and bring down any swelling. Once your muscles are nice and soft, your joints can be safely and gently guided back into their proper alignment.

Because the muscles around the joint are fully relaxed, they will not immediately pull your bones back out of place. This teamwork means your relief actually lasts.

What to Expect During Your Visit

When you come to see us, your session kicks off with a thorough assessment. Before any massage starts, our specialist will give you a proper diagnosis by looking at your posture, checking your joint mobility, and identifying your specific pain points.

  • Clothing: You stay completely clothed during the entire therapy. Just remember to wear loose and comfortable clothing.
  • Duration: A standard session lasts anywhere from 30 to 60 minutes.
  • Sensation: Since this is a deep tissue medical treatment, you will likely feel mild pressure or a dull ache as the specialist works through your muscle knots.

It is completely normal to feel a little sore for a day or two after your appointment. Once that soreness fades away, most people feel deeply energized and notice a huge drop in their pain levels.

Who Should Skip This Treatment?

Tui Na and bone setting are incredibly safe, but they are not the right fit for everyone. Please let our doctors know if you are dealing with any of the following:

  • Severe osteoporosis or broken bones that have not healed yet.
  • Open wounds, skin infections, or sores.
  • Severe heart disease, blood clots, or bleeding disorders.
  • Pregnancy, unless you are being treated by a specialist specifically trained in prenatal care.
