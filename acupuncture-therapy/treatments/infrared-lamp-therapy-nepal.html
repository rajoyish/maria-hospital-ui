What is Infrared Lamp Therapy?

Simply put, it is a painless and completely non-invasive treatment. We use special light waves to safely warm the deep tissues inside your body. You will not actually see the light, but you will definitely feel it as a deeply comforting, soothing heat.

We use two main types of infrared light:

  • Near-Infrared (NIR): This reaches deep down into your muscles, joints, and nerves to help bring down swelling and repair damaged tissues.
  • Far-Infrared (FIR): This provides a radiant warmth closer to the surface of your skin. It gets your blood flowing and helps your whole body relax.

The TDP Lamp: Much More Than Just Heat

During your treatment, we often use a specific device known as a TDP lamp. It is much more than a standard heating pad. The TDP lamp features a unique mineral plate containing over 30 essential trace minerals your body naturally needs, like zinc, iron, and calcium.

As the lamp warms up, your deep tissues absorb these minerals. This gentle process jumpstarts your cellular metabolism and natural healing in a way that regular heat just cannot match.

  • Stress and Fatigue: The warmth melts away daily tension, eases anxiety, and can even help you sleep better at night.

What to Expect During Your Session

Your comfort is always our top priority at Maria Hospital. During your session, you just get to rest comfortably on a treatment bed. Our practitioner will place the lamp at a safe distance, usually 8 to 16 inches away.

You will simply feel a pleasant, sun-like warmth for about 15 to 45 minutes. Plus, there is absolutely no recovery time needed. You can jump right back into your normal daily activities as soon as you are done!

Safety First

Infrared therapy is incredibly safe, but a proper diagnosis is key to making sure it is the right fit for you. To keep you absolutely safe, we skip this treatment if you:

  • Are pregnant, especially in your first three months.
  • Have numb or insensitive skin where you might not easily feel the heat.
  • Have active bleeding, a high fever, or a severe infection.
  • Have metal implants or a pacemaker in the area we are treating.
  • Need treatment directly over your eyes.
